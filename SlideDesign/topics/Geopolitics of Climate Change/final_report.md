The World Bank's modeling framework suggests that certain countries could experience GDP losses exceeding 10% by 2050 due to climate change, highlighting the vulnerability of economies reliant on agriculture and natural resources [1].\n\nEmerging research emphasizes that the economic impacts of climate change are not uniform. For instance, while developed nations may face lesser impacts, poorer countries are projected to suffer disproportionately due to their limited adaptive capacity [3][4].
