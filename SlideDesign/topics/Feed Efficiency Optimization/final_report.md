Two key metrics used to measure feed efficiency are the Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) and Residual Feed Intake (RFI). \n\nFCR is calculated as the amount of feed consumed divided by the weight gained. A lower FCR indicates better efficiency, as less feed is required for each unit of weight gain. For instance, advancements in livestock breeding have improved FCR from 3.5 kg feed/kg gain in the 1970s to around 2.0 kg today in top-performing farms [1][4]. \n\nRFI, on the other hand, measures the difference between actual feed intake and expected feed intake based on weight gain. This metric helps identify animals that consume less feed than expected, indicating superior efficiency [2][5]. \n\nImproving feed efficiency not only enhances profitability for farmers but also contributes to sustainability by reducing resource use and waste [3][4].\n\n\n\n![A table comparing feed efficiency metrics in livestock management.](https://leachman.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/Feed-Efficiency-Explained-300x141.jpg)\n*A table comparing feed efficiency metrics in livestock management.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Feed Efficiency - an overview | ScienceDirect Topics: https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/agricultural-and-biological-sciences/feed-efficiency  \n[2] Feed Efficiency Definition: A Complete Guide to Livestock ...: https://www.pangoo.biz/feed-efficiency-definition-a-complete-guide-to-livestock-performance-measurement-improvement/  \n[3] Feed Efficiency in Beef Cattle - Drovers: https://www.drovers.com/news/beef-production/feed-efficiency-beef-cattle  \n[4] Feed Efficiency - Vilofoss International: https://www.vilofoss.com/Our-Knowledge/Farm-Solutions/Feed-Efficiency  \n[5] What makes a “feed efficient” cow? | UMN Extension: https://extension.umn.edu/animals-and-livestock-news/what-makes-feed-efficient-cow\n\n## Factors Influencing Feed Efficiency\n\nFeed efficiency in livestock is influenced by several interrelated factors, including genetics, diet composition, health management, and environmental conditions. Genetic selection plays a crucial role, as animals with traits that enhance feed conversion ratios tend to grow faster and require less feed to reach market weight. Breeding programs focused on these traits can significantly improve productivity and reduce costs for farmers [1][5].\n\nDiet composition is equally important. A well-balanced diet that meets the specific nutritional needs of livestock at different growth stages can optimize feed efficiency.
