For instance, the British Museum utilizes VR to transport users to ancient civilizations, while AR applications enable visitors to interact with exhibits through digital overlays, enriching their understanding of the artifacts on display [1][3].\n\n3D scanning technology is also revolutionizing how museums preserve and share their collections. Institutions like The Amelia Scott are digitizing their entire collections, making them accessible for research and educational purposes [2]. AI systems can analyze visitor data to tailor experiences, making museum visits more engaging and relevant [4][5]. Additionally, advanced technologies such as 3D modeling and virtual reality offer innovative ways to document and protect heritage, making it more accessible [2].\n\nArtificial intelligence (AI) plays a crucial role in art conservation, employing techniques like hyperspectral imaging and machine learning to recover lost details in historical documents and artworks.
