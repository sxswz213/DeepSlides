One of the primary hurdles is the optimization of microbial cultures. As production volumes increase, maintaining the stability and performance of these cultures becomes critical. Variability in microbial behavior can lead to inconsistent product quality, which is a significant concern for manufacturers aiming for market acceptance and regulatory compliance [1][2].\n\nBioreactor design is another crucial factor. Traditional bioreactors may not be equipped to handle the demands of large-scale production, leading to inefficiencies and increased risk of contamination. Innovations in bioreactor technology, such as single-use systems and advanced control mechanisms, are essential for improving scalability and ensuring consistent product quality [1][4]. \n\nMoreover, the integration of data analytics and machine learning can enhance process optimization, allowing for real-time adjustments that maintain optimal fermentation conditions [5].
