In electrochemical reduction, CO₂ is typically reduced at the cathode surface through a series of multi-electron transfer reactions. The process begins with the adsorption of CO₂ onto the catalyst, followed by electron transfer and proton migration, leading to the formation of various products such as carbon monoxide (CO), formic acid (HCOOH), and hydrocarbons like methane (CH₄) and ethylene (C₂H₄) [1][4].\n\nPhotochemical reduction, on the other hand, utilizes light energy to drive the reaction. This process consists of five sequential steps: light absorption, charge separation, CO₂ adsorption, surface redox reactions, and product desorption [2]. Thermochemical methods involve high-temperature reactions that convert CO₂ into fuels and chemicals, often requiring significant energy input [3].\n\nThe choice of catalyst significantly influences the efficiency and selectivity of the reduction process. Copper-based catalysts are particularly notable for their ability to convert CO₂ into hydrocarbons and alcohols with high Faradaic efficiency, making them a focus of ongoing research [4][5].\n\n\n\n![Mechanisms of electrochemical reduction of CO₂, showcasing thermochemical and photochemical processes.](https://static.wixstatic.com/media/1cdb1d_cf87ae35a341478c9d1d480072dd0ccf~mv2.jpeg/v1/fill/w_821,h_587,al_c,q_85,usm_0.66_1.00_0.01,enc_auto/CO2+reduction+new.jpeg)\n*Mechanisms of electrochemical reduction of CO₂, showcasing thermochemical and photochemical processes.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Mechanism and Selectivity of Electrochemical Reduction of CO2 on ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9823635/  \n[2] CO2 Reduction: From the Electrochemical to Photochemical Approach: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5700640/  \n[3] A comprehensive review of electrochemical reduction of CO2 to ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2212982023000884  \n[4] Understanding the Mechanism of Electrochemical Reduction of CO2 ...: https://jmnc.samipubco.com/article_177624.html  \n[5] Fundamentals and Challenges of Electrochemical CO2 Reduction ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2451929417303996\n\n## Types of Catalysts Used in CO₂ Reduction\n\nCO₂ reduction employs various catalysts, each with unique advantages and limitations. Metal-based catalysts, such as copper and silver, are widely used due to their effectiveness in converting CO₂ into hydrocarbons. However, they often require high energy inputs and can suffer from low selectivity for desired products [1][4]. \n\nBio-inspired catalysts, derived from natural processes, offer a greener alternative. Enzymes like carbon monoxide dehydrogenase (CODH) and formate dehydrogenase (FDH) have shown promise in efficiently converting CO₂ into useful chemicals under mild conditions [1][5]. These biocatalysts, while effective, can be sensitive to environmental conditions and may require complex immobilization techniques to enhance stability and reusability [1][5].\n\nHybrid catalysts combine the strengths of both metal and bio-inspired systems, potentially improving efficiency and selectivity while reducing energy requirements [3][4]. Despite their advantages, hybrid systems can be complex to design and optimize.\n\nIn summary, the choice of catalyst in CO₂ reduction is crucial, balancing efficiency, selectivity, and environmental impact.\n\n\n\n![Illustration of different types of catalysts used in CO₂ reduction, highlighting their characteristics.](https://pub.mdpi-res.com/nanoenergyadv/nanoenergyadv-03-00004/article_deploy/html/images/nanoenergyadv-03-00004-ag.png?1678266328)\n*Illustration of different types of catalysts used in CO₂ reduction, highlighting their characteristics.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Utilization of bio-inspired catalyst for CO2 reduction into green fuels: Recent advancement and future perspectives - ScienceDirect: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2212982021003152  \n[2] Bioinspired Binickel Catalyst for Carbon Dioxide Reduction: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/jacsau.4c00047  \n[3] Hybrid Catalysts - Catalysis: https://catalysis.blog/about/index/hybrid-catalysts  \n[4] Electrocatalytic carbon dioxide reduction: from fundamental principles to catalyst design - ScienceDirect: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2590049820300217  \n[5] Bioinspired and Bioderived Aqueous Electrocatalysis - PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9999430/\n\n## Recent Advances in Green Catalysis\n\nRecent research in green catalysis for CO₂ reduction has focused on innovative materials and methods to enhance efficiency and sustainability.
