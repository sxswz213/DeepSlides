By leveraging genetic profiling and advanced technologies, these therapies aim to deliver the most effective treatments based on the unique molecular and biological features of each patient's cancer. Unlike conventional cancer treatments, which often adopt a one-size-fits-all strategy, personalized oncology focuses on delivering the right treatment to the right patient at the right time. This method utilizes genetic profiling to identify specific mutations and characteristics of a patient's cancer, allowing for targeted therapies that can improve efficacy and minimize side effects.\n\nThe principles of personalized oncology hinge on understanding the unique biological and molecular features of each patient's cancer. This includes analyzing genetic, biochemical, and lifestyle factors that influence how a patient responds to treatment. One notable example is the use of imatinib for chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) patients with the BCR-ABL mutation. This targeted therapy has significantly improved survival rates compared to traditional chemotherapy, demonstrating the effectiveness of precision medicine in hematological cancers [1].\n\nIn lung cancer, erlotinib has been successfully employed for patients with epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) mutations. Clinical trials have shown that patients receiving erlotinib experience better outcomes than those treated with standard chemotherapy, highlighting the importance of genetic profiling in treatment decisions [1][2]. Furthermore, the approval of pembrolizumab for tumors with high tumor mutational burden (TMB) exemplifies the shift towards tumor-agnostic therapies, allowing for broader application across various cancer types [1].\n\nThese case studies illustrate the potential of personalized therapies to enhance treatment efficacy and minimize adverse effects, paving the way for more individualized cancer care strategies.\n\n\n\n![Process flow of molecular profiling in personalized cancer therapies.](https://www.personalizedoncologycaremontereybay.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/inforgraphic_precision_oncology1.jpg)\n*Process flow of molecular profiling in personalized cancer therapies.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Evolution of Precision Oncology, Personalized Medicine, and Molecular Tumor Boards - PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10894322/  \n[2] Machine learning in personalized cancer treatment: https://gsconlinepress.com/journals/gscarr/sites/default/files/GSCARR-2024-0412.pdf  \n[3] Implementing Personalized Pathways for Cancer Follow-Up Care in ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7376887/  \n[4] Building Personalized Cancer Follow-up Care Pathways in the ...: https://ascopubs.org/doi/10.1200/EDBK_238267  \n[5] Personalized treatments of cancer patients: A reality in daily practice ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0305737214001248\n\n## Challenges and Future Directions\n\nPersonalized oncology faces several challenges that must be addressed to enhance its effectiveness and accessibility.
