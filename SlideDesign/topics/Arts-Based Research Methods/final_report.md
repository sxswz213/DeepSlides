By employing visual, performing, and literary arts, researchers can explore complex human experiences and convey findings in innovative ways. This report delves into the various types of arts-based research methods, their applications across disciplines such as education and health, and the challenges faced by researchers in this evolving field. Future efforts should focus on refining methodologies and ensuring ethical practices to enhance the impact of arts-based inquiry.\n\n## Types of Arts-Based Research Methods\n\nArts-based research (ABR) utilizes various artistic forms as methodologies for inquiry, primarily categorized into visual arts, performing arts, and literary arts. Visual arts, such as painting, sculpture, and photography, allow researchers to visually explore and represent complex human experiences. For example, photography can reveal hidden social issues, while painting can evoke emotional responses that enrich the research narrative.\n\nPerforming arts, including theater and dance, provide dynamic platforms for storytelling and audience engagement. These methods facilitate dialogue and foster community connections, making them particularly effective in qualitative research. Literary arts, encompassing poetry and narrative writing, enable researchers to articulate personal and collective stories, adding emotional depth to their findings.\n\nEach artistic form contributes uniquely to the research landscape, promoting a multimodal approach that captures the richness of human experience. By integrating these methods, researchers can achieve a more holistic understanding of their subjects, enhancing the overall impact of their work in qualitative research. This integration not only broadens the scope of inquiry but also deepens the emotional resonance of the findings.\n\n\n\n![An overview of different arts-based research methods, including visual arts, performative arts, and literary arts.](https://image.slidesharecdn.com/hkslidesforpodcast-crm1-170314152416/95/creative-research-methods-artsbased-methods-3-638.jpg?cb=1489507101)\n*An overview of different arts-based research methods, including visual arts, performative arts, and literary arts.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Arts-based Methods in Socially Engaged Research Practice: A Classification Framework. https://www.researchgate.net/publication/363679201_Arts-based_Methods_in_Socially_Engaged_Research_Practice_A_Classification_Framework  \n[2] Arts-based Methods in Socially Engaged Research Practice. https://journals.library.ualberta.ca/ari/index.php/ari/article/view/27370  \n[3] Two decades of using arts-based methods in community-based research. https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1747938X17300301  \n[4] Arts-Based Research as a Qualitative Methodology - In education, ABR methods such as photo-elicitation and collaborative arts projects have been shown to enhance student engagement and understanding. For instance, a study highlighted how children participating in a performance developed a deeper comprehension of narratives through multi-modal representations (Barton & Baguley, 2014) [3]. \n\nIn health research, arts-based methods facilitate the expression of complex emotions and experiences, allowing participants to communicate in ways that traditional verbal methods may not capture. For example, children in a hospital setting used drawing to articulate their ideal hospital environment, providing valuable insights into their needs and preferences (Lambert et al., 2014) [3]. \n\nMoreover, ABR promotes inclusivity by amplifying marginalized voices and fostering empathy among participants and researchers alike. A primary concern is the perceived lack of rigorous validation processes, which often leads to skepticism regarding the reliability of ABR compared to traditional research methods [1][4]. This skepticism is compounded by the subjective nature of artistic interpretation, resulting in varied understandings of the same artistic output, which can undermine the research's validity [2][4].\n\nEthical considerations are crucial in ABR. Maintaining participant confidentiality is particularly challenging when visual arts are involved, as sharing artistic products may inadvertently reveal personal information [4]. Additionally, the technical skills required for certain arts-based methods can limit participation, especially among individuals without prior artistic training [4][5]. \n\nDespite these challenges, ABR can yield profound insights into complex human experiences. Addressing these limitations through careful methodological design and ethical considerations is essential for the advancement of arts-based research [2][5].\n\n\n\n![Four steps outline the process of art-based research, including spending time with subjects, understanding their experience, creating artifacts, and generating embodied insights.](https://image.slidesharecdn.com/artinquallilasrandrianarivony-180311045733/85/utilizing-artbased-research-as-qualitative-research-method-5-638.jpg?cb=1667567407)\n*Four steps outline the process of art-based research, including spending time with subjects, understanding their experience, creating artifacts, and generating embodied insights.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Underpinnings, Issues and Challenges of Art-based Enquiry: https://pesaagora.com/access-archive-files/ACCESSAV32N1N2_057.pdf  \n[2] View of Creative and Arts-Based Research Methods in ...: https://www.qualitative-research.net/index.php/fqs/article/view/2961/4227  \n[3] The potentials and challenges of arts-based research: https://www.researchgate.net/figure/The-potentials-and-challenges-of-arts-based-research_fig1_341125470  \n[4] The Benefits and Limitations of Arts-Based Approaches to ...: https://youthrex.com/blog/the-benefits-and-limitations-of-arts-based-approaches-to-evaluation/  \n[5] Going against the grain?
