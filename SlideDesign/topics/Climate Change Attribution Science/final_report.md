This field has evolved significantly since its inception in the late 20th century, driven by advancements in climate modeling and observational data. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) first introduced the concept of attribution in its 1990 report, emphasizing the need to link climate change to specific causes, primarily human-induced factors.\n\nHistorically, early studies focused on identifying the \"fingerprint\" of human influence on climate, using statistical methods to analyze temperature records. Over time, the methodology has expanded to include probabilistic approaches, which assess how much more likely certain extreme weather events are due to climate change. For instance, studies have shown that extreme heat events are now significantly more frequent and intense due to anthropogenic warming.
