The primary types include public transport, private transport, and multimodal transport systems. \n\nPublic transport, such as buses and trains, plays a crucial role in enhancing accessibility, especially for those without access to private vehicles. It provides an affordable and efficient means of travel, reducing traffic congestion and environmental impact. Studies indicate that improved public transport can significantly increase non-motorized travel and decrease reliance on cars, particularly in urban areas where walking and cycling infrastructure is also developed [1][2].\n\nPrivate transport, primarily through automobiles, remains popular due to its convenience and speed. However, it often leads to increased traffic and pollution. The challenge lies in balancing the use of private vehicles with sustainable alternatives [3][4].\n\nMultimodal transport systems integrate various modes of transport, allowing seamless transitions between them. This approach enhances overall accessibility and efficiency, catering to diverse travel needs. For instance, a well-designed multimodal system can facilitate easy transfers between buses, trains, and bike-sharing services, promoting a shift away from car dependency [5].\n\nIn summary, effective transport connectivity requires a blend of public, private, and multimodal systems to enhance accessibility and sustainability.\n\n\n\n![An integrated multimodal transportation system showcasing various components like road traffic monitoring and public information.](https://image.slidesharecdn.com/fromtransportsystemstomobility-thales-150401090314-conversion-gate01/95/from-transport-systems-to-mobility-management-thales-13-638.jpg?cb=1427879065)\n*An integrated multimodal transportation system showcasing various components like road traffic monitoring and public information.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Measuring Accessibility for Multimodal Public Transport: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1226798825003083  \n[2] Introduction to Multi-Modal Transportation Planning: https://www.vtpi.org/multimodal_planning.pdf  \n[3] Multimodal Transportation: What Is It?: https://www.streetlightdata.com/what-is-multimodal-transportation/  \n[4] Multimodal Transportation: What Tech Powers Journeys?: https://intellias.com/multimodal-transportation/  \n[5] Guidebook for Measuring Multimodal Network Connectivity: https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/environment/bicycle_pedestrian/publications/multimodal_connectivity/\n\n## Impact of Connectivity on Urban Development\n\nTransport networks play a crucial role in urban development, influencing economic growth and social equity.
