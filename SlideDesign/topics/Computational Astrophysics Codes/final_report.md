Among the most significant techniques are N-body simulations, hydrodynamics, and radiative transfer.\n\nN-body simulations are crucial for studying gravitational interactions in systems with many bodies, such as galaxies. These simulations help researchers understand the dynamics of star clusters and the formation of large-scale structures in the universe. They utilize advanced algorithms to calculate the gravitational forces acting on each body, allowing for the exploration of various scenarios in cosmic evolution [1][2].\n\nHydrodynamics is another essential technique, focusing on the behavior of fluids in astrophysical contexts. This method is vital for modeling phenomena like star formation and supernova explosions, where gas dynamics play a critical role. Coupled with radiative transfer, hydrodynamic simulations can accurately depict how energy is transported through different media, providing insights into the thermal and chemical evolution of astrophysical objects [3][4].\n\nTogether, these techniques form the backbone of computational astrophysics, enabling scientists to simulate and analyze the universe's complex processes.\n\n\n\n![A vibrant 3D representation of an N-body simulation displaying cosmic structures and gravitational interactions.](https://i.ytimg.com/vi/jiTzg0uupco/maxresdefault.jpg)\n*A vibrant 3D representation of an N-body simulation displaying cosmic structures and gravitational interactions.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Computational Astrophysics | U-M LSA Astronomy: https://lsa.umich.edu/astro/research/computational-astrophysics.html  \n[2] Computational Astrophysics | Department of Astrophysical Sciences: https://web.astro.princeton.edu/research/computational-astrophysics  \n[3] Computational astrophysics - Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_astrophysics  \n[4] Efficient radiative transfer techniques in hydrodynamic simulations: https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/478/3/3478/4998870\n\n## Applications of Computational Codes\n\nComputational astrophysics codes play a crucial role in advancing our understanding of various astrophysical phenomena, including galaxy formation, stellar evolution, and cosmology.
