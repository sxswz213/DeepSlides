It encompasses various strategies designed to handle variations in system parameters and unmodeled dynamics, which are common in robotic applications.\n\nKey principles of robust control include understanding different types of uncertainties, balancing robustness with performance, and utilizing stability margins. Techniques such as H-infinity control and sliding mode control are widely used to ensure that a system remains stable and performs well under varying conditions. H-infinity control minimizes the maximum gain of the system's transfer function, making it less sensitive to uncertainties, while sliding mode control drives system states onto a predefined surface, enhancing robustness against disturbances.\n\nThe significance of robust control lies in its ability to ensure reliable operation in real-world environments, where conditions can change unpredictably.
