Each agency has distinct responsibilities but collaborates under the Coordinated Framework for the Regulation of Biotechnology, established in 1986 and updated in 2017.\n\nThe FDA is responsible for ensuring the safety and effectiveness of food, drugs, and biological products derived from biotechnology. It regulates products under the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act, focusing on human and animal health. The USDA, particularly through its Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS), oversees agricultural biotechnology, ensuring that products do not pose risks to plant and animal health. The EPA regulates biotechnology products that may impact the environment, particularly under the Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act.\n\nThese agencies work together to streamline regulatory processes, enhance public confidence, and ensure that biotechnology innovations are safe for consumers and the environment. In the United States, the regulatory framework is relatively centralized, allowing for quicker approvals of biotechnological products. This difference often leads to longer timelines for the introduction of new biotechnologies in the EU compared to the US [1][3].\n\nIndia's regulatory framework for genetically modified (GM) technology presents its own complexities, influenced by both local agricultural needs and international standards.
