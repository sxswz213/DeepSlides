Among the most prominent frameworks are LIME, SHAP, the What-If Tool, and AIX360.\n\n**LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations)** provides local explanations for any classifier by approximating the model's behavior around a specific prediction. It achieves this by perturbing the input data and observing the resulting changes in predictions, making it versatile across various model types and data formats [1][2].\n\n**SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)** leverages cooperative game theory to assign each feature an importance value for a given prediction. This method ensures that the contributions of all features are accounted for, providing consistent and interpretable explanations, especially for complex models like deep learning networks [1][3].\n\n**The What-If Tool (WIT)**, developed by Google, offers an interactive interface for analyzing machine learning models without requiring extensive coding. It allows users to visualize model behavior, conduct counterfactual analysis, and assess fairness metrics, making it accessible for both experts and non-experts [2][4].\n\n**AIX360 (AI Explainability 360)** is an open-source toolkit from IBM that encompasses a variety of algorithms for model interpretability. It addresses the challenges of neural network interpretability and provides tools for both post-hoc explanations and direct interpretability [1][3].\n\nThese frameworks collectively contribute to building trust and accountability in AI systems, facilitating their adoption in critical applications.\n\n\n\n![Comparative overview of explainable AI techniques, focusing on LIME and SHAP methods.](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/228fe08f-6144-4cd5-a27b-44630dc5829d_4303x2471.png)\n*Comparative overview of explainable AI techniques, focusing on LIME and SHAP methods.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Top 10 Explainable AI (XAI) Frameworks - MarkTechPost: https://www.marktechpost.com/2024/04/24/top-10-explainable-ai-xai-frameworks/  \n[2] Explainable AI Frameworks - Securing.AI: https://securing.ai/ai-security/explainable-ai-frameworks/  \n[3] 6 Explainable AI (XAI) Frameworks for Transparency in AI: https://dev.to/amananandrai/6-explainable-ai-xai-frameworks-for-transparency-in-ai-3koj  \n[4] Break open the Black Box: Explainable AI frameworks to increase transparency: https://medium.com/@shreyalunkad/break-open-the-black-box-explainable-ai-frameworks-to-increase-transparency-a07b171ee10e\n\n## Applications of Explainable AI\n\nExplainable AI (XAI) is increasingly vital across various sectors, particularly in healthcare, finance, and autonomous vehicles. One significant issue is the inherent complexity of deep learning models. These models, such as convolutional neural networks, consist of numerous parameters and layers, making it difficult to trace how inputs lead to specific outputs. This complexity often results in a \"black box\" scenario, where the decision-making process is opaque, complicating the task of providing clear, understandable explanations to users [1].\n\nAnother challenge is the trade-off between accuracy and interpretability. While complex models can achieve high accuracy, they often sacrifice transparency. For instance, a deep learning model may outperform simpler models in tasks like medical diagnosis, but if clinicians cannot understand its reasoning, they may be reluctant to trust its predictions [1][3]. Additionally, the lack of standardized evaluation methods for XAI complicates the assessment of explanation quality. Different techniques may yield conflicting results, and without a consensus on evaluation criteria, developers struggle to select reliable methods [1][4].\n\nAddressing these challenges requires a concerted effort to develop robust evaluation frameworks and to tailor explanations to meet the specific needs of users across various domains.\n\n\n\n![Different approaches to model interpretability in Explainable AI.](https://www.altexsoft.com/static/blog-post/2023/11/d090b3d9-1ee8-43d6-bbc6-1f063b60b54c.jpg)\n*Different approaches to model interpretability in Explainable AI.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] What are the challenges in applying Explainable AI to deep learning? - Milvus: https://milvus.io/ai-quick-reference/what-are-the-challenges-in-applying-explainable-ai-to-deep-learning  \n[2] Explainable AI in 2025 - Nitor Infotech: https://www.nitorinfotech.com/blog/explainable-ai-in-2025-navigating-trust-and-agency-in-a-dynamic-landscape/  \n[3] Transparent AI: The Case for Interpretability and Explainability - arXiv: https://arxiv.org/html/2507.23535v1  \n[4] Survey on Explainable AI: Techniques, challenges and open issues - ScienceDirect: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S095741742401577X\n\n# Explainable AI Frameworks\n\nThe rapid advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies has led to increased complexity in AI systems, often resulting in a lack of transparency in their decision-making processes.
