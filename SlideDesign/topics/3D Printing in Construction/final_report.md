This innovative technology utilizes computer-aided design (CAD) and building information modeling (BIM) to guide the printing process, allowing for precise and complex designs. The primary methods of 3D printing in construction include extrusion, powder bonding, and additive welding.\n\nExtrusion is the most common technique, where materials like concrete or polymers are deposited through a nozzle to form walls and other components. Powder bonding involves using powdered materials that are fused together using a binding agent or heat, suitable for creating intricate designs. Additive welding, on the other hand, is used for metal components, where layers of metal are welded together to form robust structures.\n\nMaterials used in 3D printing for construction range from concrete and plastics to metals and composites, enabling a wide variety of applications, including entire buildings, structural components, and architectural models.
