Smallsats, which are defined as satellites weighing less than 500 kg, are revolutionizing the industry due to their lower costs and faster deployment times. In 2022, smallsats accounted for 96% of all satellites launched, highlighting their growing dominance in the market [2][5].\n\nMoreover, the development of low Earth orbit (LEO) constellations is enhancing global connectivity and data collection capabilities. Companies like SpaceX and OneWeb are leading the charge in deploying large networks of smallsats, which provide high-throughput, low-latency broadband services, particularly in underserved regions [3][4]. This shift not only democratizes access to satellite technology but also supports various applications, from environmental monitoring to disaster response.\n\nAdditionally, advancements in satellite communication technologies, such as high-throughput satellites (HTS) and optical communication systems, are expected to further enhance data transmission capabilities.
