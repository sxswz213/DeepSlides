During the Little Ice Age, for instance, Pieter Bruegel the Elder created winter landscapes that captured the harsh realities of colder climates. His work, \"The Hunters in the Snow,\" illustrates not only the beauty of winter but also the struggles of daily life during this period of climatic change [4].\n\nThe colors and themes in paintings from the 18th and 19th centuries reveal insights into atmospheric conditions. Research indicates that the deep reds in sunsets painted by artists like Caspar David Friedrich can be linked to pollution levels, suggesting that artists inadvertently recorded environmental changes [1]. Similarly, de Loutherbourg's *Coalbrookdale by Night* (1802) presents a fiery industrial scene that starkly contrasts with earlier pastoral ideals, highlighting the environmental consequences of industrial progress.\n\nThese artistic reflections not only document changing landscapes but also raise awareness of the social and environmental issues stemming from industrialization.
