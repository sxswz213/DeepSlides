Its primary objective is to assess the overall value of a technology by comparing its costs and benefits, which aids in determining its commercial viability. TEA employs various methodologies, including cost benchmarking, which involves comparing the costs of a new technology against existing commercial technologies to evaluate competitiveness and market readiness.\n\nThe importance of cost benchmarking in TEA cannot be overstated. It allows analysts to identify whether a new technology can achieve cost parity with established alternatives, which is crucial for successful commercialization. This process involves estimating both capital expenses (CapEx) and operating expenses (OpEx), providing a comprehensive view of the financial implications of adopting new technologies. CapEx refers to one-time costs associated with acquiring assets such as equipment and facilities. These costs are amortized over the asset's useful life to relate them to specific production volumes. Conversely, OpEx encompasses recurring costs, including materials, labor, and energy, which can be classified as either fixed or variable depending on their relationship to production output.\n\nThe significance of functional units in TEA cannot be overstated. They provide a basis for comparing different technologies by standardizing the metrics used in cost assessments. This is particularly important when technologies differ in size or function, ensuring that comparisons are meaningful and relevant. By employing a performance-equivalent functional unit, analysts can achieve \"apples-to-apples\" comparisons, enhancing the reliability of the economic evaluations conducted.\n\n### Sources\n[1] Introduction to Techno-Economic Analysis: https://www.energy.gov/sites/default/files/2022-01/2022-01-19%20-%20Intro%20to%20TEA%20-%20Slides%20and%20Transcript_compliant_1_0.pdf  \n[2] A techno-economic and environmental evaluation of the ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0360319924042174  \n[3] Techno-economic analysis to identify the optimal ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0196890423005769  \n[4] Techno-Economic Analysis and Optimization of Gray and ...: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.energyfuels.4c05806  \n[5] Techno-Economic Assessment Guidelines for CO2 Utilization: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/energy-research/articles/10.3389/fenrg.2020.00005/full\n\n\n\n![A comparison chart outlining the differences between Capital Expenses (CapEx) and Operating Expenses (OpEx).](https://i.pinimg.com/736x/95/98/4f/95984f99351ced5be80999238960b974.jpg)\n*A comparison chart outlining the differences between Capital Expenses (CapEx) and Operating Expenses (OpEx).*\n\n\n## Techno-Economic Analysis Tools and Resources\n\nTechno-economic analysis (TEA) is essential for evaluating the economic viability of various industrial processes.
