This practice is particularly beneficial in situations where a physical examination may not be feasible. However, for veterinarians to prescribe treatment via telemedicine, a Veterinarian-Client-Patient Relationship (VCPR) must be established. This relationship ensures that the veterinarian has sufficient knowledge of the animal's health history and can make informed decisions regarding care [1][3].\n\nTelemedicine is often confused with telehealth and teletriage. Telehealth is a broader term that encompasses all remote health services, including education and general advice, while telemedicine specifically refers to the diagnosis and treatment of patients [4][5]. Teletriage, on the other hand, is a preliminary assessment to determine if an animal requires immediate veterinary attention, which can be conducted without a prior VCPR [1][2]. Understanding these distinctions is crucial for pet owners seeking veterinary care through digital platforms.\n\n\n\n![A veterinarian provides remote veterinary telemedicine consultation with a pet owner through a tablet.](https://reboundog.news/content/images/2022/09/telemedicine.jpg)\n*A veterinarian provides remote veterinary telemedicine consultation with a pet owner through a tablet.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] A New Era Of Care: Telemedicine in the Veterinary Industry: https://albertaanimalhealthsource.ca/content/new-era-care-telemedicine-veterinary-industry  \n[2] Veterinary Telemedicine: https://www.canadianveterinarians.net/media/ililtnnn/cvma-veterinary-telemedicine-guidelines.pdf  \n[3] Guidelines for the Use of Telemedicine in Veterinary Practice: https://www.cvbc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/CVBC-Guidelines-Update-Telemedicine-July-2021-FINAL-for-posting.pdf  \n[4] What is Veterinary Telehealth and Telemedicine?: https://vettriage.com/what-is-veterinary-telehealth-and-telemedicine/  \n[5] A comprehensive guide to understanding veterinary telehealth: https://www.dvm360.com/view/a-comprehensive-guide-to-understanding-veterinary-telehealth\n\n## Types of Veterinary Telemedicine Platforms\n\nVeterinary telemedicine platforms can be categorized into three main types: Basic DIY, Plug & Play, and Premium options. Many individuals face barriers to traditional veterinary care, such as geographic isolation, mobility issues, or financial constraints. Telemedicine can bridge these gaps, allowing pet owners to consult with veterinarians from home, which is particularly beneficial for seniors, disabled individuals, and those living in underserved areas [2][3]. Additionally, virtual consultations can alleviate the stress associated with transporting pets to clinics, especially for anxious or aggressive animals [4].\n\nHowever, challenges persist, particularly regarding the establishment of the veterinarian-client-patient relationship (VCPR). This regulatory barrier can hinder the potential benefits of telemedicine, as it restricts veterinarians from providing timely care and advice remotely.
