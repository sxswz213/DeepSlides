Quantum mechanics, for instance, relies heavily on linear algebra and functional analysis, particularly the use of Hilbert spaces. In this context, physical observables are represented as operators, and the states of quantum systems are described by wave functions, which are elements of these Hilbert spaces. The mathematical formulation of quantum mechanics allows for the prediction of measurable quantities through eigenvalues and eigenstates of these operators, fundamentally altering our understanding of physical reality compared to classical mechanics [1][2].\n\nRelativity, on the other hand, introduces a geometric perspective to physics, where spacetime is treated as a four-dimensional manifold. The mathematical formulation involves tensors and differential geometry, allowing for the description of gravitational phenomena as curvature in spacetime [3]. Statistical mechanics bridges the gap between microscopic and macroscopic systems, employing probability theory to describe the behavior of large ensembles of particles, often using concepts from calculus and combinatorics [4].\n\nThese theories, while distinct, share a common goal: to provide a coherent mathematical description of the physical universe, illustrating the deep interplay between mathematics and physics.\n\n\n\n![Mathematical formulation of quantum mechanics, highlighting key concepts such as Hilbert space and operators.](https://image.slidesharecdn.com/linearalgebraseminar-130123090418-phpapp01/85/Mathematical-Formulation-of-Quantum-Mechanics-10-320.jpg)\n*Mathematical formulation of quantum mechanics, highlighting key concepts such as Hilbert space and operators.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Mathematical formulation of quantum mechanics - Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_formulation_of_quantum_mechanics  \n[2] Nine formulations of quantum mechanics: https://faculty1.coloradocollege.edu/~dhilt/hilt44211/AJP_Nine%20formulations%20of%20quantum%20mechanics.pdf  \n[3] Quantum Theory and Mathematical Rigor: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qt-nvd/  \n[4] Quantum mechanics as classical statistical mechanics with an ontic ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5670234/\n\n## Interdisciplinary Applications\n\nMathematical physics plays a crucial role in various fields, including engineering, biology, and computer science.
