When exposed to light, QDs can absorb photons, exciting electrons from the valence band to the conduction band, which leads to the emission of light at specific wavelengths. This property allows QDs to produce pure colors, making them ideal for enhancing display technologies, particularly in liquid crystal displays (LCDs) and light-emitting diodes (LEDs) [1][5].\n\nIn display applications, QDs can be used in two main ways: as a photoluminescent layer that converts blue light from LEDs into red and green light, or as electroluminescent materials in quantum dot light-emitting diodes (QLEDs). The former improves color accuracy and brightness in LCDs, while the latter allows for self-emissive displays where each pixel generates its own light [1][2][4]. QLED, which utilizes quantum dots to enhance color accuracy and brightness, is essentially an LCD with a quantum dot layer. This technology excels in bright environments, providing vibrant colors and high peak brightness, making it suitable for HDR content. However, it struggles with deep blacks and contrast due to its reliance on a backlight, which can lead to blooming effects around bright objects.\n\nOn the other hand, QD-OLED combines the best features of OLED and quantum dots. Each pixel in a QD-OLED display emits its own light, allowing for true blacks and infinite contrast ratios. The addition of quantum dots enhances color reproduction, resulting in a more vivid and accurate color palette. While QD-OLED displays are generally more expensive, they offer superior performance in both dark and bright viewing conditions, making them a compelling choice for high-end applications.\n\nIn summary, while QLED provides excellent brightness and color in well-lit rooms, QD-OLED stands out for its superior contrast and color accuracy, positioning it as a strong competitor against traditional OLED displays.\n\n\n\n![Comparison of quantum dot technologies in LCD and OLED displays.](https://fthmb.tqn.com/n6ktIZExMxyX__ieIesgjkZmOCQ=/768x0/filters:no_upscale()/lcdtv-wquantumdots-vs-oled-5806d42d3df78cbc287a9880.jpg)\n*Comparison of quantum dot technologies in LCD and OLED displays.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] QLED vs. OLED: What's the Difference and Which Is Better? - CNET: https://www.cnet.com/tech/home-entertainment/qled-vs-oled-whats-the-difference-and-which-is-better/  \n[2] Comparing Display Technologies: TFT LCD, OLED, and Quantum Dot: https://rjoytek.com/comparing-display-technologies-tft-lcd-oled-and-quantum-dot/  \n[3] QLED vs OLED: The Differences That Actually Matter: https://www.smarthomesounds.co.uk/blog/faqs/qled-vs-oled-everything-you-need-to-know  \n[4] QLED vs. OLED (and QD-OLED): Which TV Tech Is Right for You?: https://www.consumerreports.org/electronics-computers/tvs/qled-vs-oled-and-qd-oled-which-tv-tech-is-right-for-you-a6691090566/  \n[5] OLED vs. LCD: A Comprehensive Comparison - Riverdi: https://riverdi.com/blog/oled-vs-lcd-a-comprehensive-comparison?srsltid=AfmBOorSA7n9NtD7xS04AyKYq1EOHJu2DBbavP12Nmm5x7ZZdlrizYY\n\n## Future Trends in Quantum Dot Displays\n\nQuantum dot (QD) technology is poised to revolutionize display systems across various sectors, including augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), and portable devices.
