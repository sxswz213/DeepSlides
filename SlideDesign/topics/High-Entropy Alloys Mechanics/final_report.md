This composition results in a significant increase in configurational entropy, which stabilizes the solid solution phase, typically exhibiting either body-centered cubic (bcc) or face-centered cubic (fcc) structures. The term \"high-entropy alloy\" was first introduced by Jien-Wei Yeh, highlighting the importance of entropy in their formation and stability [1][3].\n\nHEAs are characterized by four core effects: high entropy, severe lattice distortion, sluggish diffusion, and the cocktail effect. The high entropy effect enhances solid solution formation, leading to simpler microstructures than expected. Severe lattice distortion arises from the diverse atomic sizes and bonding energies of the constituent elements, which can influence mechanical and thermal properties. Sluggish diffusion refers to the reduced atomic mobility within the alloy, while the cocktail effect emphasizes the synergistic enhancement of properties due to the multi-element composition [2][4][5].\n\nThese characteristics make HEAs promising for various applications, particularly in extreme environments where traditional alloys may fail.\n\n\n\n![Applications of high-entropy alloys in different industries.](https://image.slidesharecdn.com/hea-180123061308/95/high-entropy-alloys-26-638.jpg?cb=1516688040)\n*Applications of high-entropy alloys in different industries.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] High Entropy Alloys - an overview: https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/materials-science/high-entropy-alloys  \n[2] Full article: High-Entropy Alloys: A Critical Review: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/21663831.2014.912690  \n[3] High-entropy alloy: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-entropy_alloy  \n[4] A critical review of high entropy alloys and related concepts: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1359645416306759  \n[5] Exploring high entropy alloys: A review on thermodynamic ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11605370/\n\n## Mechanical Properties of High-Entropy Alloys\n\nHigh-entropy alloys (HEAs) exhibit remarkable mechanical properties that distinguish them from traditional alloys. Processing Techniques\n\nHigh-entropy alloys (HEAs) are synthesized using various methods that significantly influence their microstructure and properties. One of the primary techniques is mechanical alloying, which involves high-energy ball milling of elemental powders to achieve a homogeneous mixture at the nanoscale. This non-equilibrium process allows for the creation of unique microstructures that enhance the mechanical properties of the alloys [2][3].\n\nConsolidation techniques, such as spark plasma sintering (SPS) and conventional press and sintering, are crucial for transforming the milled powders into dense bulk materials. These methods help in achieving the desired mechanical properties and microstructural integrity [1][4]. Additionally, liquid mixing methods, including electric arc melting and induction melting, are employed to produce HEAs in bulk form, allowing for better control over the alloy's composition and phase formation [5].\n\nThe choice of synthesis and processing techniques directly impacts the final properties of HEAs, such as strength, ductility, and corrosion resistance, making it essential to optimize these methods for specific applications [3][4].\n\n\n\n![Flowchart illustrating various synthesis methods for high-entropy alloys (HEAs).](https://www.frontiersin.org/files/Articles/519854/fenrg-08-00073-HTML-r1/image_m/fenrg-08-00073-g001.jpg)\n*Flowchart illustrating various synthesis methods for high-entropy alloys (HEAs).*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Mechanically alloyed high entropy alloys: existing challenges and ... - https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2238785422001417  \n[2] High entropy alloys synthesized by mechanical alloying: A review - https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2949917825000203  \n[3] Structural Particularities, Prediction, and Synthesis Methods in High ... - https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/14/17/7576  \n[4] High entropy alloys and methods for synthesis - https://www.matec-conferences.org/articles/matecconf/pdf/2024/13/matecconf_icmr2024_14002.pdf  \n[5] High entropy alloys and methods for synthesis - ResearchGate - https://www.researchgate.net/publication/383457506_High_entropy_alloys_and_methods_for_synthesis\n\n## Applications of High-Entropy Alloys\n\nHigh-entropy alloys (HEAs) are emerging as promising materials in various industries due to their unique properties.
