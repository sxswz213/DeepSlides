It is characterized by a layered hexagonal structure, similar to graphite, but unlike graphite, hBN is an excellent electrical insulator with a wide bandgap of approximately 5.9 eV. This makes it suitable for various electronic applications, particularly as a dielectric material in transistors and other semiconductor devices [3][5].\n\nhBN exhibits exceptional mechanical strength and thermal stability, making it ideal for high-temperature applications. Its thermal conductivity ranges from 200 to 400 W/m·K in-plane, while it remains chemically inert in harsh environments, resisting oxidation up to 1000°C [4][5]. These properties enable hBN to be used in advanced composites, solid lubricants, and thermal management systems, enhancing the performance of materials in electronics and aerospace applications [2][3].\n\nIn summary, hBN's unique combination of electrical insulation, thermal stability, and mechanical strength positions it as a critical material in the development of next-generation electronic devices and high-performance composites.\n\n\n\n![Electrical properties of hBN in FET structures](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/359139658/figure/fig4/AS:11431281179390001@1691182301372/Electrical-properties-of-hBN-Gr-MoS2-hBN-G-MoS2-FET-and-hBN-Gr-hBN-G-FET-structures.png)\n*Electrical properties of hBN in FET structures*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Electrical properties and applications of graphene, hexagonal boron ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2542529317300597  \n[2] Mechanical and electronic properties of boron nitride nanosheets ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9042849/  \n[3] Hexagonal Boron Nitride (h-BN): Structure, Properties, and ...: https://www.samaterials.com/content/hexagonal-boron-nitride-h-bn-structure-properties-and-applications.html  \n[4] On functional boron nitride: Electronic structures and thermal ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2772949422000055  \n[5] Boron Nitride | Machinable Ceramic Grades & Properties: https://precision-ceramics.com/materials/boron-nitride/\n\n## MXenes and Xenes\n\nMXenes are a class of two-dimensional (2D) materials derived from transition metal carbides, nitrides, and carbonitrides. They exhibit remarkable properties such as high electrical conductivity, lightweight structure, and tunable surface chemistry, making them ideal candidates for various applications, particularly in electromagnetic interference (EMI) shielding. Recent studies have shown that MXenes can effectively block electromagnetic radiation, which is crucial in the context of increasing electronic device usage and the associated electromagnetic pollution [1][2].\n\nThe advancements in MXene technology have led to the development of nitrogen-substituted MXenes, which demonstrate exceptional high-frequency EMI shielding capabilities. These materials can operate efficiently in the sub-terahertz range while maintaining minimal thickness and weight, outperforming traditional metallic shields [5]. Furthermore, MXenes are being explored for their potential in laser technology, where their unique properties can enhance performance in various applications [4].\n\nOverall, the versatility and performance of MXenes position them as promising materials for future electronic and communication technologies.\n\n\n\n![Illustration of the electromagnetic interference shielding capabilities of MXenes and Xenes, highlighting their atomic structure and electron distribution.](https://d1otjdv2bf0507.cloudfront.net/images/news/ImageForNews_39836_1666717818771108.png)\n*Illustration of the electromagnetic interference shielding capabilities of MXenes and Xenes, highlighting their atomic structure and electron distribution.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] MXenes for electromagnetic interference shielding: Experimental and theoretical perspectives.
