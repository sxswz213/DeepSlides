Key methods include optical, near-infrared (NIR), thermal infrared (TIR), and LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging).\n\nOptical remote sensing captures images in the visible spectrum, allowing archaeologists to identify surface features and patterns. This technique has been instrumental in discovering ancient sites obscured by vegetation or urban development. For instance, high-resolution optical imagery has revealed thousands of unmarked tombs and settlements in Egypt, showcasing its effectiveness in archaeological prospection [1][3].\n\nNIR remote sensing is particularly sensitive to vegetation health, making it useful for detecting buried structures that affect plant growth. This method has successfully identified ancient Roman villas and agricultural systems by analyzing crop marks that appear in NIR imagery [3][4].\n\nTIR remote sensing detects heat emitted from the Earth's surface, revealing subsurface features based on temperature differences. This technique has uncovered extensive Bronze and Iron Age settlements in the Middle East, where buried structures retain heat differently than surrounding soils [3][4].\n\nLiDAR technology uses laser pulses to create detailed 3D maps of landscapes, stripping away vegetation to expose hidden archaeological features. This method has been pivotal in mapping ancient cities, such as those in the Maya lowlands, providing insights into past human activities and environmental interactions [2][5].\n\nIn summary, these remote sensing techniques offer archaeologists powerful tools for site discovery, mapping, and understanding historical landscapes without disturbing the ground.\n\n\n\n![Illustration of the electromagnetic spectrum with highlighted sections for various remote sensing techniques.](https://pub.mdpi-res.com/remotesensing/remotesensing-16-01671/article_deploy/html/images/remotesensing-16-01671-g001.png?1715237344)\n*Illustration of the electromagnetic spectrum with highlighted sections for various remote sensing techniques.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] A Review of Satellite Remote Sensing Techniques and Their Applications in Archaeology: https://globaldigitalheritage.org/research/a-review-of-satellite-remote-sensing-techniques-and-their-applications-in-archaeology/  \n[2] Remote Sensing Methods: Techniques & Data | Vaia: https://www.vaia.com/en-us/explanations/archaeology/archaeology-documentation/remote-sensing-methods/  \n[3] Interacting with the Artificial Intelligence (AI) Language: https://www.mdpi.com/2571-9408/6/5/214  \n[4] Remote Sensing for Archaeological Applications: https://www.intechopen.com/chapters/45147  \n[5] 12.2 Remote sensing and LiDAR applications: https://library.fiveable.me/the-archaeology-of-southeast-asia/unit-12/remote-sensing-lidar-applications/study-guide/XwnO31iIbIeIK2qH\n\n## Comparison of Remote Sensing Techniques\n\nRemote sensing has transformed archaeological research by providing non-invasive methods to detect and analyze hidden sites.
