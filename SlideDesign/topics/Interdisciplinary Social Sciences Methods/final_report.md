Initially, positivism dominated, emphasizing empirical observation and quantitative analysis to establish cause-and-effect relationships. This approach, rooted in the natural sciences, often overlooked the subjective dimensions of human experience [1][4].\n\nAs critiques of positivism emerged, scholars recognized the limitations of purely quantitative methods. This led to the rise of qualitative research, which focuses on understanding the meanings individuals attach to their actions and experiences. Interpretivism gained traction as researchers sought to explore the complexities of social reality [2][5]. \n\nThe integration of qualitative and quantitative methods culminated in the development of mixed methodologies. This approach allows researchers to triangulate data, providing a more comprehensive understanding of social phenomena by combining the strengths of both paradigms [1][3]. The evolution of these methods illustrates a broader recognition of the complexity of social realities and the need for diverse methodological frameworks in social sciences.\n\n\n\n![A human hand reaching toward a vibrant, multicolored, abstract light display symbolizing interdisciplinary approaches in social sciences.](https://epacis.net/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/interdisciplinary_science_overview_8270.jpg)\n*A human hand reaching toward a vibrant, multicolored, abstract light display symbolizing interdisciplinary approaches in social sciences.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Hasani, M. Each approach offers unique contributions to social science research.\n\nMulti-disciplinary research involves collaboration among experts from different fields, where each discipline addresses a common issue from its perspective. For instance, a project on public health may include insights from sociology, psychology, and economics, enhancing the understanding of health behaviors. This approach allows for a richer analysis of complex social issues by integrating diverse viewpoints.\n\nCross-disciplinary research examines one discipline through the lens of another. An example is using psychological principles to inform architectural design, which can lead to environments that promote well-being. This method encourages innovative solutions by applying concepts from one field to another.\n\nTrans-disciplinary research integrates knowledge from various fields and includes non-academic stakeholders, fostering a comprehensive understanding of complex issues. This approach is crucial for tackling global challenges like climate change, where solutions require diverse insights and collaborative efforts. The choice of approach significantly influences research outcomes, highlighting the importance of breaking down disciplinary barriers to effectively address complex social issues.\n\n\n\n![A tree diagram outlining ten tips for developing interdisciplinary socio-ecological researchers.](https://i2insights.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/kelly_10-tips-next-generation-interdisciplinary-research.jpg)\n*A tree diagram outlining ten tips for developing interdisciplinary socio-ecological researchers.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Why, with whom, and how to conduct interdisciplinary ...: https://academic.oup.com/spp/article/52/2/165/7907587  \n[2] Inter-, Cross-, and Transdisciplinarity: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-78062-2_4  \n[3] Inter, Multi, Cross, Trans, & Intra-disciplinary: https://www.archpsych.co.uk/post/disciplinarities-definitions  \n[4] transcending and transgressing research methods in: https://bristoluniversitypressdigital.com/gsc/view/journals/gscj/4/1/article-p9.xml  \n[5] Cross-disciplinary research - STIP Compass - OECD: https://stip.oecd.org/stip/interactive-dashboards/themes/TH26  \n[6] Interdisciplinary Projects in Social Science: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/interdisciplinary-projects-social-science-dr-dinesh-chauhan  \n[7] 15 new interdisciplinary and exploratory research projects: https://www.u-bordeaux.fr/en/news/15-new-interdisciplinary-and-exploratory-research-projects\n\n## Mixed Methods in Interdisciplinary Research\n\nMixed methods research combines qualitative and quantitative approaches to provide a comprehensive understanding of complex social phenomena. This integration allows researchers to explore the breadth of data through quantitative measures while delving into the depth of human experiences via qualitative insights. The synergy between these methodologies enhances the validity and richness of research findings, making it particularly valuable in interdisciplinary studies where diverse perspectives are essential.\n\nFor instance, interdisciplinary mixed-methods systematic reviews synthesize knowledge from various fields, addressing challenges and successes in combining different research methodologies. This approach not only facilitates a more nuanced understanding of social issues but also fosters collaboration among researchers from different disciplines, enhancing engagement and innovation in research design. By employing mixed methods, researchers can triangulate data, ensuring that findings are robust and reflective of the complexities inherent in social science research.\n\nThe growing recognition of mixed methods as a distinct research paradigm underscores its importance in advancing knowledge across disciplines, ultimately contributing to more effective solutions to societal challenges.\n\n\n\n![A visual representation of mixed-method research design, illustrating the integration of qualitative and quantitative data.](https://www.inonafrica.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/Mixed-Method.png)\n*A visual representation of mixed-method research design, illustrating the integration of qualitative and quantitative data.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Interdisciplinary mixed methods systematic reviews: Reflections on methodological best practices, theoretical considerations, and practical implications across disciplines - ScienceDirect: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2590291122000493  \n[2] The Use of Mixed-Methods Research in Social Sciences: https://falconediting.com/en/blog/the-use-of-mixed-methods-research-in-social-sciences/  \n[3] Mixed methods research: what it is and what it could be: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11186-019-09345-5  \n[4] Mixed Methods Research | Advantages & Applications - ATLAS.ti: https://atlasti.com/guides/the-guide-to-mixed-methods-research/mixed-methods-advantages  \n[5] Benefits and challenges of engaging Majority World children in ...: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13645579.2022.2153977\n\n# Interdisciplinary Social Sciences Methods\n\n## Introduction\n\nThe report explores the significance of interdisciplinary methods in social sciences, emphasizing their evolution and application in addressing complex social issues.
