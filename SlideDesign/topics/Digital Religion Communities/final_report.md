The rise of digital platforms has reshaped community dynamics, allowing for broader participation and enhanced access to religious resources. Historically, religious knowledge was often confined to institutional authorities, requiring physical presence for engagement. However, the emergence of online platforms has democratized access to religious content, enabling individuals to explore diverse interpretations and practices beyond traditional confines.\n\nThe COVID-19 pandemic accelerated this shift, as many religious communities transitioned to virtual gatherings and online rituals. Research indicates that this digital transition has redefined religious observance, fostering new forms of community and spiritual engagement. Online services have become commonplace, allowing individuals to participate in worship from home, thus challenging the necessity of physical attendance. This evolution has led to a more fluid understanding of religious identity, where individuals can engage with multiple faith traditions simultaneously, a phenomenon often referred to as \"spiritual tinkering\" or syncretism.\n\nDespite these advancements, challenges persist, including concerns about authenticity and the potential dilution of traditional practices. A significant concern is the tension between authenticity and the mediated nature of online interactions. Privacy issues and algorithmic biases further complicate matters, often alienating marginalized groups and underscoring the need for inclusivity [3][4].\n\nDespite these challenges, the digital realm offers innovative pathways for religious engagement. Initiatives like the UN's Global Digital Compact aim to foster inclusive digital spaces, enabling religious communities to leverage technology for deeper connections and broader accessibility [4].
