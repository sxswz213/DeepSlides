In poultry nutrition, they are primarily used as direct-fed microbials (DFMs) to enhance gut health and improve overall performance. Common probiotic strains include Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, and Bacillus species, which help maintain a balanced intestinal microbiota, reduce pathogenic bacteria, and enhance nutrient absorption [1][2][3].\n\nThe classification of probiotics can be based on their origin, such as bacterial versus non-bacterial (yeast), and their form, including spore-forming and non-spore-forming types. Multi-species probiotics combine various strains to maximize health benefits, while single-species probiotics focus on the effects of one specific strain [2][4]. \n\nIncorporating probiotics into poultry diets has been shown to improve feed conversion ratios, growth rates, and egg production, while also providing immune support against enteric pathogens [2][5]. As the poultry industry moves away from antibiotic growth promoters, probiotics offer a promising alternative to enhance animal health and productivity.\n\n\n\n![Probiotics improve bone health and nutrient absorption in poultry.](https://www.allaboutfeed.net/app/uploads/2022/09/IMG_AAF07_Probiotics_figure1-768x377.jpg)\n*Probiotics improve bone health and nutrient absorption in poultry.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] The Role of Probiotics in the Poultry Industry - PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2812824/  \n[2] Overview on probiotics and their impact in commercial poultry: https://www.netjournals.org/pdf/MRI/2020/2/20-014.pdf  \n[3] Probiotics in poultry feed: A comprehensive review - Abd El‐Hack: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jpn.13454  \n[4] Probiotics in Animal Nutrition - FAO Knowledge Repository: https://openknowledge.fao.org/server/api/core/bitstreams/2fda2226-b000-4cee-8186-8cacab86316b/content  \n[5] Overview of the Use of Probiotics in Poultry Production - PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8230106/\n\n## Mechanisms of Action of Probiotics\n\nProbiotics play a crucial role in enhancing gut health in poultry through several mechanisms. One primary mechanism is competitive exclusion, where probiotics inhibit the growth of harmful pathogens by occupying binding sites in the gastrointestinal tract and competing for nutrients. This process is facilitated by the production of antimicrobial compounds such as bacteriocins, organic acids, and hydrogen peroxide, which create an unfavorable environment for pathogens (Source 1, Source 4).\n\nAdditionally, probiotics modulate the immune response, enhancing both innate and adaptive immunity. They stimulate the secretion of immunoglobulin A (IgA), which is vital for gut immunity, and promote the activity of immune cells (Source 2, Source 3). Furthermore, probiotics improve the integrity of the gut barrier, reducing intestinal permeability and preventing the translocation of pathogens into the bloodstream (Source 4).\n\nOverall, the incorporation of probiotics in poultry nutrition not only supports gut health but also enhances nutrient absorption and growth performance, making them a valuable alternative to antibiotics in poultry production (Source 1, Source 3).\n\n\n\n![Mechanisms of action of probiotics in the poultry gastrointestinal tract.](https://cdnintech.com/media/chapter/68801/1512345123/media/F1.png)\n*Mechanisms of action of probiotics in the poultry gastrointestinal tract.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] The Role of Probiotics in the Poultry Industry - PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2812824/  \n[2] Effect of Spore-Forming Probiotics on the Poultry Production: A Review: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9647184/  \n[3] Probiotics in poultry: a comprehensive review: https://basicandappliedzoology.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s41936-024-00379-5  \n[4] Probiotics in Poultry: Unlocking Productivity Through Microbiome Modulation and Gut Health: https://www.mdpi.com/2076-2607/13/2/257\n\n## Benefits of Probiotics in Poultry\n\nProbiotics play a crucial role in enhancing poultry health and productivity. Research indicates that probiotics can strengthen the immune system, reducing disease susceptibility and enhancing overall well-being in chickens [1][2]. \n\nAdditionally, probiotics have been shown to improve performance during heat stress, a common challenge in poultry production. They help maintain gut integrity and reduce inflammation, which can be exacerbated by high temperatures [3][4]. Key strains include *Lactobacillus spp.*, *Bacillus subtilis*, *Bifidobacterium spp.*, *Enterococcus faecium*, and *Saccharomyces boulardii*. \n\n*Lactobacillus spp.* is known for its ability to enhance gut health and reduce intestinal infections, leading to improved nutrient absorption. *Bacillus subtilis* increases villus height in the intestines, which strengthens the gut barrier and enhances feed conversion ratios. Meanwhile, *Bifidobacterium spp.* contributes to lowering cholesterol levels and boosting immunity. *Enterococcus faecium* helps reduce pathogen loads, while *Saccharomyces boulardii* mitigates oxidative stress, particularly under challenging conditions such as heat stress.\n\nThe application of these probiotics can be through feed, water, or in ovo inoculation, making them versatile tools in poultry farming. Their use not only supports animal health but also aligns with the growing demand for antibiotic-free poultry products, promoting sustainable farming practices.\n\n\n\n![A hen surrounded by eggs, illustrating the benefits of probiotic feed supplements for improving digestive health in poultry.](https://refitanimalcare.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/probiotics-for-poultry.webp)\n*A hen surrounded by eggs, illustrating the benefits of probiotic feed supplements for improving digestive health in poultry.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Enhancing Poultry Nutrition with Probiotics: A Comprehensive Insight - https://www.glamac.com/blog/enhancing-poultry-nutrition-with-probiotics-a-comprehensive-insight/  \n[2] The Role of Probiotics in the Poultry Industry - PMC - https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2812824/  \n[3] Applications of Probiotics in Poultry: Enhancing Immunity and Beneficial Effects on Production Performances and Health - https://www.researchgate.net/publication/229596530_Applications_of_Probiotics_in_Poultry_Enhancing_Immunity_and_Beneficial_Effects_on_Production_Performances_and_Health_-_A_Review  \n[4] The Application of Probiotics and Prebiotics in Poultry - https://www.mdpi.com/2673-8392/5/1/35  \n[5] Improving quality of poultry and its meat products with probiotics, prebiotics and phytoextracts - https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0032579123008064\n\n## Challenges and Future Directions\n\nThe integration of probiotics into poultry nutrition presents several challenges that need to be addressed for optimal effectiveness. One significant issue is strain specificity; not all probiotic strains yield the same benefits, and their efficacy can vary based on the poultry species and environmental conditions. This necessitates further research to identify the most effective strains for specific applications in poultry farming [1][2].\n\nCost-effectiveness is another critical challenge. While probiotics can enhance growth performance and health, their initial costs may deter some producers, especially in regions with tight profit margins.
