They utilize sensors, actuators, and communication networks to enable real-time interaction between the digital and physical worlds. This integration allows CPS to monitor and control physical processes, making them essential in various industries such as manufacturing, healthcare, and transportation.\n\nThe architecture of CPS typically consists of several key components: physical processes, sensors that collect data, computational nodes that analyze this data, and actuators that execute commands based on the analysis. This structure facilitates a feedback loop where data is continuously gathered and processed, allowing for autonomous decision-making and optimization of operations.\n\nCPS applications are diverse, ranging from smart manufacturing systems that enhance production efficiency to autonomous vehicles that navigate safely using real-time data.
