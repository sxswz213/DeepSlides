The most common types include workshops, mentoring, online courses, and collaborative learning opportunities.\n\nWorkshops are structured sessions that provide teachers with targeted training on specific topics, such as classroom management or innovative teaching strategies. These can be periodic and often involve hands-on activities to engage participants actively [1][2]. Mentoring programs pair experienced educators with newer teachers, fostering a supportive environment for sharing best practices and addressing challenges [5]. \n\nOnline courses have gained popularity due to their flexibility, allowing teachers to learn at their own pace. Platforms like Coursera and Alison offer a range of courses covering essential teaching skills and contemporary educational trends [3][4].
