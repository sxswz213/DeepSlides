These standards define key terminology, establish performance measures, and outline quality assurance protocols. They cover various aspects of the manufacturing process, including the qualification of machine operators, system performance, and the properties of finished parts.\n\nThe ISO/ASTM 52900 series provides a comprehensive framework for AM technologies, detailing requirements for materials, processes, and equipment. For instance, ISO/ASTM 52920 focuses on quality assurance measures throughout the manufacturing process, ensuring that products meet industry specifications and customer expectations. Additionally, standards like ASTM F3572-22 and ISO/ASTM 52941-20 address specific applications in aerospace and medical fields, highlighting the importance of tailored guidelines for different sectors.\n\nBy adhering to these standards, manufacturers can enhance the reliability and consistency of their products, ultimately fostering trust in additive manufacturing technologies.\n\n\n\n![A hierarchical chart illustrating the structure of additive manufacturing standards, including categories such as feedstock materials, process/equipment, and finished parts.](https://www.engineering.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/AM_Standards_Structure_jznbmv.jpg)\n*A hierarchical chart illustrating the structure of additive manufacturing standards, including categories such as feedstock materials, process/equipment, and finished parts.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Additive Manufacturing Standards - Standards Products: https://store.astm.org/products-services/standards-and-publications/standards/additive-manufacturing-standards.html  \n[2] ISO/ASTM 52920:2023(en), Additive manufacturing: https://www.iso.org/obp/ui/en/#!iso:std:76911:en  \n[3] 10.5 Standards and certifications in additive manufacturing: https://library.fiveable.me/additive-manufacturing-and-3d-printing/unit-10/standards-certifications-additive-manufacturing/study-guide/XFdCcbv2qwLmbuwu  \n[4] Quality Assurance in Additive Manufacturing: https://www.a3dmfg.com/blog/quality-assurance-additive-manufacturing  \n[5] ISO/ASTM 52920 Standard - Additive Manufacturing: https://www.tuvsud.com/en-us/industries/manufacturing/machinery-and-robotics/additive-manufacturing/ref/column/iso-52920\n\n## Key Organizations and Their Roles\n\nAdditive manufacturing (AM) standards are primarily developed by two key organizations: ASTM International and the International Organization for Standardization (ISO). ASTM International has been at the forefront of AM standards development since 2009, establishing the Committee on Additive Manufacturing Technologies (F42). This committee comprises over 500 members from 25 nations, focusing on creating standards that enhance the quality and functionality of AM processes and materials. Notable standards include guidelines for evaluating mechanical properties and specifications for AM file formats, which are crucial for ensuring consistency and reliability in AM applications across various industries, including aerospace and medical sectors [1][2].\n\nISO collaborates closely with ASTM to create joint standards, ensuring global acceptance and usability. Their partnership, formalized through a Partner Standards Developing Organization agreement, aims to streamline the development process and reduce redundancy in standards creation [1][4].
