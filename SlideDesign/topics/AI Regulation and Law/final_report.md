This framework categorizes AI technologies into four risk levels: unacceptable, high, limited, and minimal risk. Each category has specific compliance requirements aimed at ensuring safety and protecting fundamental rights.\n\n1. **Unacceptable Risk**: This category includes AI applications that pose a significant threat to safety and rights, such as social scoring systems and real-time biometric identification in public spaces. These systems are strictly prohibited.\n\n2. **High Risk**: High-risk AI systems, which include those used in critical sectors like healthcare and law enforcement, are subject to stringent regulations. Providers must conduct risk assessments, ensure transparency, and implement human oversight mechanisms.\n\n3. **Limited Risk**: AI systems in this category, such as chatbots, require transparency, meaning users must be informed they are interacting with AI.\n\n4. **Minimal Risk**: This includes applications like spam filters and entertainment software, which face no specific regulatory requirements but must still adhere to general EU laws.\n\nThe Act aims to balance innovation with accountability, fostering a safe environment for AI development while addressing ethical concerns.\n\n\n\n![Classification chart outlining the risk levels and compliance requirements for AI systems under the EU AI Act.](https://kajabi-storefronts-production.kajabi-cdn.com/kajabi-storefronts-production/file-uploads/blogs/2147552113/images/828d3-18d5-2c8-e26-8d8b57d2623_EU_AI_ACT_-_Risk_based_compliance.png)\n*Classification chart outlining the risk levels and compliance requirements for AI systems under the EU AI Act.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] RISK CLASSIFICATIONS OF AI IN THE EU ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE ACT: https://cyberjustice.blog/2025/01/31/risk-classifications-of-ai-in-the-eu-artificial-intelligence-act/  \n[2] EU AI Act: Risk-Classifications of the AI Regulation - Trail-ML: https://www.trail-ml.com/blog/eu-ai-act-how-risk-is-classified  \n[3] High-level summary of the AI Act | EU Artificial Intelligence Act: https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/high-level-summary/  \n[4] Understanding the EU AI Act - ISACA: https://www.isaca.org/resources/white-papers/2024/understanding-the-eu-ai-act  \n[5] EU AI Act: first regulation on artificial intelligence | Topics: https://www.europarl.europa.eu/topics/en/article/20230601STO93804/eu-ai-act-first-regulation-on-artificial-intelligence\n\n## Challenges in AI Regulation\n\nRegulating artificial intelligence (AI) presents significant challenges due to the rapid pace of technological advancement and the complexity of ethical considerations.
