SAFIR, a nonlinear finite element software, models the behavior of structures under fire conditions, allowing engineers to analyze various materials and geometries. This capability is crucial for understanding how different structures respond to heat and stress, ultimately leading to improved safety measures and design practices.\n\nThe software enables the simulation of temperature distributions and structural deformations, providing insights into potential failure modes. By integrating fire dynamics with structural analysis, SAFIR supports performance-based design approaches, which focus on achieving specific safety outcomes rather than merely adhering to prescriptive codes. This flexibility fosters innovation in architectural design while ensuring that safety standards are met.\n\nAs fire safety engineering continues to evolve, tools like SAFIR will play a pivotal role in developing resilient structures capable of withstanding fire hazards, thereby enhancing overall public safety.\n\n\n\n![A digital model of a building's framework alongside an active fire incident, demonstrating the application of SAFIR in fire safety engineering.](https://engineering.jhu.edu/safir/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/SAFIR-website_homepage_3-scaled.jpg)\n*A digital model of a building's framework alongside an active fire incident, demonstrating the application of SAFIR in fire safety engineering.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] SAFIR - Multi-Hazard Resilient Structures - Johns Hopkins University: https://mars.jhu.edu/safir/  \n[2] SAFIR – A finite element software for analyzing structures in fire: https://engineering.jhu.edu/safir/  \n[3] SAFIR – Home: https://safirsupport.be/index.php/safir/  \n[4] Advances in Performance-Based Design for Structures in Fire: https://www.sfpe.org/publications/periodicals/sfpeeuropedigital/sfpeeurope32/europeissue32feature2  \n[5] SAFIR - GiD SIMULATION: https://www.gidsimulation.com/downloads/safir/\n\n## Collaborative Efforts in Fire Safety Research\n\nThe SFPE Foundation plays a pivotal role in advancing fire safety research through collaborative initiatives and funding opportunities.
