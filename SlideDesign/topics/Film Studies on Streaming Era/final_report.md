With platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime, and Disney+, audiences now have unprecedented access to a vast library of films, leading to a significant decline in theater attendance. This shift has enabled independent and niche films to reach audiences that may not have been accessible through traditional theatrical releases, fostering greater diversity in content [2]. Platforms like Netflix and Amazon Prime Video generate steady income through monthly fees, allowing for greater investment in original content and diverse storytelling. This shift has enabled filmmakers to explore innovative narratives without the constraints of theatrical performance expectations, fostering a more creative environment.\n\nHowever, the revenue-sharing model in streaming can be unpredictable. Unlike traditional studios that benefit from box office earnings, streaming services often license content, which can lead to lower financial returns for filmmakers.
