This unique structure allows for high atomic utilization and uniform active sites, which enhances catalytic efficiency compared to traditional catalysts that often rely on larger metal clusters or nanoparticles. The active sites in SACs are typically anchored to the support through strong metal-support interactions, which can influence their electronic properties and catalytic behavior significantly [1][2].\n\nThe properties of SACs differ markedly from conventional catalysts. For instance, SACs exhibit high selectivity and stability due to their unique geometric and electronic configurations. This allows them to perform efficiently in various reactions, including hydrogen production and environmental remediation [3][4]. Moreover, the ability to manipulate the coordination environment around the active sites enables researchers to tailor the catalytic properties for specific applications, making SACs a versatile tool in modern catalysis [5].\n\nIn summary, the fundamental principles of SACs lie in their atomic-level design, which provides enhanced performance and opens new avenues for catalytic applications.\n\n\n\n![Diagram illustrating the role of a single-atom nickel (Ni) catalyst in various chemical reactions.](https://pub.mdpi-res.com/catalysts/catalysts-13-00845/article_deploy/html/images/catalysts-13-00845-ag.png?1683509253)\n*Diagram illustrating the role of a single-atom nickel (Ni) catalyst in various chemical reactions.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Coordination structures design of single-atom catalysts for ... - https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2950477525000023  \n[2] Single–Atom Supported Catalysts and Beyond - Wiley Online Library - https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adma.202504518  \n[3] Single‐Atom Long‐Range Interaction: Basic Principles and ... - https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adsu.202300564?af=R  \n[4] Rational design principles of single-atom catalysts for hydrogen ... - https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlehtml/2024/ee/d4ee02763a  \n[5] Research Progress and Application of Single-Atom Catalysts - https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8587903/\n\n## Synthesis Methods of SACs\n\nSingle-atom catalysts (SACs) can be synthesized using two primary approaches: top-down and bottom-up methods. The top-down approach involves breaking down bulk materials into single atoms through mechanical or chemical processes. Techniques such as mechanical crushing and thermal treatment are commonly employed to achieve this. In contrast, the bottom-up approach builds SACs from atomic or molecular precursors, allowing for greater control over the atomic arrangement and coordination environment of the catalyst. This method often utilizes chemical vapor deposition or sol-gel processes to assemble atoms into desired structures.\n\nRecent advancements have introduced innovative techniques, such as 3D printing, which streamline the synthesis of SACs by allowing for customizable designs and efficient production. This method combines metal precursors with polymer inks, enabling the direct fabrication of SACs with specific geometries. The choice of synthesis method significantly impacts the performance and stability of SACs, making it crucial to select the appropriate technique based on the intended application.\n\n\n\n![Comparison of top-down and bottom-up synthesis methods for single-atom catalysts.](https://cdnintech.com/media/chapter/71200/1512345123/media/F1.png)\n*Comparison of top-down and bottom-up synthesis methods for single-atom catalysts.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Recent Advances in Single-Atom Catalysts for High ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1452398125002068  \n[2] Large-scale synthesis of functional single-atom catalysts: https://www.nature.com/articles/s42004-023-00834-4  \n[3] Top-down synthetic strategies toward single atoms on the ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2590238521006408  \n[4] Rapid Screening of Single-Atom Catalyst Synthesis ...: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.analchem.5c00351  \n[5] Strategies for enhancing catalytic performance of single-...: https://www.the-innovation.org/article/doi/10.59717/j.xinn-mater.2025.100141\n\n## Characterization Techniques\n\nCharacterizing single-atom catalysts (SACs) is crucial for understanding their catalytic properties and mechanisms. Various advanced techniques are employed to confirm the presence and distribution of single atoms within these catalysts. Key methods include Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy (EDX), Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy (EELS), and Extended X-ray Absorption Fine Structure (EXAFS) spectroscopy. EDX and EELS are particularly useful for determining the elemental composition and electronic structure of SACs, while EXAFS provides insights into the coordination environment of the active sites [1][3][4].\n\nAdditionally, X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS) is employed to analyze the oxidation states of metal atoms, which significantly influence the activity and stability of SACs [3][4].
