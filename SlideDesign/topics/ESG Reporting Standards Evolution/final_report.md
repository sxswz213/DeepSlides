The launch of the Pax World Fund in 1971, the first ethical mutual fund, set a precedent for socially responsible investing (SRI) by avoiding investments linked to the Vietnam War [1]. This era also saw the establishment of regulatory frameworks, such as the National Environmental Policy Act in the U.S., which compelled companies to disclose their environmental impacts.\n\nThe 1990s marked a significant evolution in ESG reporting with the creation of the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) in 1997, which provided guidelines for sustainability reporting [2]. The early 2000s witnessed a surge in ESG adoption, driven by scandals like Enron and the recognition of ESG factors as material to financial performance. The United Nations' Principles for Responsible Investment (PRI), launched in 2006, further solidified ESG's role in investment strategies [3]. By the 2010s, ESG reporting became mainstream, with mandatory reporting requirements emerging in various jurisdictions, including the European Union [4]. \n\nToday, ESG reporting is integral to corporate strategy, reflecting a shift towards greater transparency and accountability in business practices.\n\n\n\n![A timeline illustrating the key milestones in the development of ESG reporting standards from the 1970s to the present.](https://www.msci.com/documents/1296102/17835852/202005_History-ESG-Indexes_Desktop_V2.png/60918a3f-1af9-6b0f-7238-910e363503e6)\n*A timeline illustrating the key milestones in the development of ESG reporting standards from the 1970s to the present.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] A brief history of ESG: From pioneer to mainstream: https://www.thecorporategovernanceinstitute.com/insights/guides/a-brief-history-of-esg-from-pioneer-to-mainstream/?srsltid=AfmBOoqjZMWM8EpOuEvOms7MXSiS7QAr89uhqv5PbXQZJEBG8ikNaR08  \n[2] The Global ESG Reporting Movement: A Historical Overview: https://iriscarbon.com/the-global-esg-reporting-movement-a-historical-overview/  \n[3] Environmental, Social, and Corporate Governance: A History of ESG: https://sites.asit.columbia.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/29/2023/05/Wang-Luna_thesis.pdf  \n[4] The Evolution of ESG: A Historical Timeline: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/evolution-esg-historical-timeline-ashok-kumhar-wqjqc\n\n## Key ESG Reporting Frameworks\n\nThe evolution of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting has led to the establishment of several key frameworks, each serving distinct purposes. The Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) focuses on comprehensive sustainability reporting, emphasizing transparency and stakeholder engagement. It covers a wide range of topics, making it suitable for organizations aiming for broad accountability [1][2].\n\nIn contrast, the Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB) provides industry-specific standards that prioritize financially material sustainability information. This framework is tailored for investors, offering metrics that help assess the financial implications of ESG factors [2][4]. SASB's approach allows companies to communicate relevant data effectively, enhancing comparability within industries.\n\nThe Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) zeroes in on climate-related risks and opportunities, guiding companies in disclosing how climate change impacts their financial performance. TCFD's recommendations encourage organizations to integrate climate considerations into their governance and risk management processes [3][5].\n\nTogether, these frameworks support organizations in meeting diverse stakeholder expectations and regulatory requirements, fostering a culture of sustainability and accountability in corporate reporting.\n\n\n\n![Comparison of key ESG reporting frameworks highlighting their primary purposes and audiences.](https://optimole.movingworlds.org/w:auto/h:auto/q:mauto/https://blog.movingworlds.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/11/a_comparison_of_frameworks.jpg)\n*Comparison of key ESG reporting frameworks highlighting their primary purposes and audiences.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Mastering ESG reporting: https://www.thecorporategovernanceinstitute.com/insights/guides/mastering-esg-reporting-frameworks-standards-and-best-practices/?srsltid=AfmBOorFRI_URWoTRy43dVHb2JUeWdDcXz8IQDOUHiVDGcbByZr-gpG_  \n[2] Introduction to Popular ESG Reporting Frameworks: https://www.esystems.fi/en/blog/introduction-to-popular-esg-reporting-frameworks-like-gri-sasb-and-tcfd  \n[3] ESG Reporting Evolution: Comparing GRI, SASB, and TCFD: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/esg-reporting-evolution-comparing-gri-sasb-tcfd-dr-choen-krainara-ngqac  \n[4] 4 ESG Reporting Frameworks & Standards: https://novisto.com/resources/blogs/four-popular-esg-reporting-schemes-that-complement-each-other  \n[5] TCFD vs SASB: Key ESG Framework Differences: https://senecaesg.com/insights/tcfd-vs-sasb-key-esg-framework-differences-explained-2025/\n\n## Current Trends and Challenges in ESG Reporting\n\nThe landscape of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting is rapidly evolving, driven by technological advancements, regulatory changes, and increasing stakeholder demands.
