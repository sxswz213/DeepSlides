Political instability and violence disrupt communities, hindering the transmission of languages to future generations. This is particularly evident in conflict zones, where language loss often accompanies social disintegration and forced migration [2][3]. \n\nEconomic pressures also contribute significantly to language decline. As communities undergo socioeconomic changes, individuals may adopt dominant languages that promise better opportunities, leading to a decrease in the use of their native tongues [4][5]. Furthermore, negative perceptions of minority languages can exacerbate this trend, as speakers may view their languages as inferior or less valuable [1][4]. \n\nGlobalization complicates this dynamic by promoting dominant cultures and languages at the expense of smaller, indigenous ones. Migration for work or education often results in individuals abandoning their native languages in favor of those that facilitate communication in new environments [1][4]. The lack of institutional support for minority languages, such as inadequate educational resources and language policies, further threatens their survival [1][3][5]. \n\nIn summary, the decline of endangered languages is driven by a multifaceted interplay of political, economic, and social factors, creating a challenging environment for their survival.\n\n\n\n![A world map illustrating the number of languages classified as threatened or endangered in 2022 by region.](https://cdn.statcdn.com/Infographic/images/normal/26867.jpeg)\n*A world map illustrating the number of languages classified as threatened or endangered in 2022 by region.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] 15.3 Endangered languages and language preservation - Fiveable: https://library.fiveable.me/introduction-linguistics/unit-15/endangered-languages-language-preservation/study-guide/SaGYo5MSVfNMzafs  \n[2] Language Endangerment and Political Instability (LEPI): https://ci.unt.edu/corsal/research/lepi.html  \n[3] Language Endangerment and Political Instability - UNT Digital Library: https://digital.library.unt.edu/explore/collections/LEPI/  \n[4] SOCIOECONOMIC CHANGE AND LANGUAGE ENDANGERMENT: https://kuscholarworks.ku.edu/server/api/core/bitstreams/329d2c0d-9f5c-4b16-a0b5-631a5607c7cd/content  \n[5] Endangered Languages | Ethnologue Free: https://www.ethnologue.com/endangered-languages/  \n[6] Language Endangerment: A Threat to Cultural Diversity: https://www.numberanalytics.com/blog/language-endangerment-guide  \n[7] Understanding Language Endangerment and Its Impact on Culture: https://www.cliffsnotes.com/study-notes/27696749  \n[8] Why care about endangered languages? | SIL Global: https://www.sil.org/about/endangered-languages/why-care-about-endangered-languages\n\n## Classification of Endangered Languages\n\nUNESCO classifies endangered languages into several categories based on their level of endangerment. These categories are crucial for understanding the vitality of a language and the urgency of revitalization efforts. The classifications include:\n\n1. **Safe/Not Endangered**: Languages that are spoken by all generations with uninterrupted intergenerational transmission.\n2. **Vulnerable**: Languages that are mostly spoken by children, but may be restricted to certain domains, such as home.\n3. **Definitely Endangered**: Languages that children no longer learn as their mother tongue at home.\n4. **Severely Endangered**: Languages spoken primarily by the grandparental generation, with little to no use among parents.\n5. **Critically Endangered**: Languages where the youngest speakers are grandparents or older, and they speak the language infrequently.\n6. **Extinct**: Languages with no living speakers left.\n\nThis classification helps in assessing the health of languages and prioritizing efforts for documentation and revitalization, as many languages are at risk of disappearing due to globalization and cultural shifts [1][2][5].\n\n\n\n![UNESCO's classification criteria for endangered languages, illustrating levels from Critically Endangered to Extinct.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Lang_Status_List.svg/684px-Lang_Status_List.svg.png)\n*UNESCO's classification criteria for endangered languages, illustrating levels from Critically Endangered to Extinct.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] What are criteria used by UNESCO to put a language into four levels of language endangerment?
