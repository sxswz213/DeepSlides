At the core of this architecture are edge devices, such as sensors and IoT devices, which collect data and perform initial processing. This localized processing reduces latency and bandwidth usage, enabling immediate decision-making without relying on cloud connectivity[1][3].\n\nEdge servers play a crucial role by aggregating data from multiple edge devices and executing more complex AI algorithms. They serve as intermediaries that enhance processing capabilities while maintaining low latency. One of the primary issues is hardware limitations. Edge devices often have constrained processing power and memory, making it difficult to deploy complex AI models that require significant computational resources. This necessitates model optimization techniques, such as quantization and sparsity, to ensure that AI applications can run efficiently on these devices [1][5].\n\nData management is another critical challenge. Edge AI systems must handle data locally, which can complicate data synchronization and consistency across devices.
