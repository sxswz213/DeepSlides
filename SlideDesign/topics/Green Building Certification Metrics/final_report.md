It operates on a point-based system, with certification levels ranging from Certified to Platinum [1][4].\n\nBREEAM, originating in the UK, assesses buildings across nine categories, including energy, water, and health. Key among these are energy efficiency, water usage, and waste management. \n\nEnergy efficiency is often measured by final and primary energy use, expressed in kilowatt-hours per square meter per year (kWh/m²/year). This metric helps assess how effectively a building utilizes energy compared to local baselines or building codes. Effective water management strategies, such as low-flow fixtures and rainwater harvesting, are crucial for reducing potable water use [2][4].\n\nWaste management metrics evaluate the amount of construction waste that is minimized, reused, or recycled. This is often expressed as a percentage of total waste generated during construction or renovation processes [1][2]. \n\nThese metrics not only help in achieving certification but also contribute to long-term sustainability goals by promoting resource efficiency and reducing environmental impact.\n\n\n\n![LEED certification categories emphasizing energy efficiency and environmental design.](https://primedatacenters.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/technial-the-building-of-a-LEED-certified-green-data-center-1_en.gif)\n*LEED certification categories emphasizing energy efficiency and environmental design.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Suggested Impact Reporting Metrics for Green Building Projects: https://www.icmagroup.org/assets/documents/Regulatory/Green-Bonds/Resource-Centre/Final-Green-Buildings-Reporting-Metrics-March-2019-including-Reporting-Templates-200319.pdf  \n[2] 12 Essential Green Building Certification Requirements: https://buildinggreenshow.com/12-essential-green-building-certification-requirements/  \n[3] The impact of green building certifications on GRESB ratings for in-use buildings: https://www.gresb.com/nl-en/the-impact-of-green-building-certifications-on-gresb-ratings-for-in-use-buildings/  \n[4] Green Building Standards and Certification Systems: https://www.wbdg.org/resources/green-building-standards-and-certification-systems  \n[5] LEED rating system | U.S. Green Building Council: https://www.usgbc.org/leed\n\n## Comparison of Certification Systems\n\nGreen building certification systems, such as LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) and BREEAM (Building Research Establishment Environmental Assessment Method), play a crucial role in promoting sustainable construction practices. LEED, developed in the United States, emphasizes a comprehensive approach that includes energy efficiency, water conservation, and sustainable materials selection. Its user-friendly framework is particularly appealing for new construction projects. However, it has been criticized for lacking ongoing monitoring and recertification, which raises concerns about its long-term effectiveness [1][2].\n\nConversely, BREEAM, originating in the UK, adopts a more localized approach, integrating specific regional environmental considerations. This adaptability allows BREEAM to address local challenges effectively, making it suitable for a variety of building types, including existing structures. Its rigorous assessment process enhances accountability and transparency in sustainability practices [1][3]. \n\nBoth systems share the goal of reducing environmental impacts and promoting occupant well-being, yet they differ significantly in their methodologies and implementation strategies.
