The four primary types include transradial, transhumeral, transtibial, and transfemoral prosthetics, each designed to meet specific functional needs.\n\n**Transradial prosthetics** are used for individuals who have lost their forearm and hand. These devices typically feature a snug-fitting socket and can incorporate advanced technologies, such as myoelectric controls, allowing users to grasp and manipulate objects effectively.\n\n**Transhumeral prosthetics** replace the entire arm, including the elbow. These prosthetics are more complex, often integrating powered joints to facilitate movement. The lightweight nature of 3D-printed materials enhances comfort and usability.\n\n**Transtibial prosthetics** are designed for those with below-knee amputations. They consist of a socket, pylon, and foot, with designs that mimic natural leg functions, providing stability and shock absorption during walking.\n\n**Transfemoral prosthetics** replace the leg above the knee. These devices may feature microprocessor-controlled knees that adapt to different terrains, enhancing mobility and user experience. The customization offered by 3D printing ensures a precise fit, improving overall comfort and functionality.\n\nThe advancements in 3D printing technology allow for rapid production and personalization, making prosthetics more accessible and effective for users.\n\n\n\n![Diagram of 3D-printed prosthetic designs for transradial and transfemoral limbs, highlighting socket fitting and loading directions.](https://pub.mdpi-res.com/materials/materials-16-01985/article_deploy/html/images/materials-16-01985-g003.png?1677571473)\n*Diagram of 3D-printed prosthetic designs for transradial and transfemoral limbs, highlighting socket fitting and loading directions.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] 3D Printed Prosthetics: What They Are and How They Work - UPTIVE: https://uptivemfg.com/3d-printed-prosthetics/  \n[2] 3D Printed Prosthetics: Manufacturing Process, Materials, and Types - Thomasnet: https://www.thomasnet.com/insights/3d-printed-prosthetics/  \n[3] Advancements in Prosthetics with a Sense of Touch (2025) - Is Brave: https://isbrave.com/guides/prosthetics-with-a-sense-of-touch/  \n[4] 3D printed personalized assistive devices: A material, technique ... - ScienceDirect: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352940724003482  \n[5] What Are the Different Types of Prosthetics? - Protech Ortho: https://www.protechortho.com/what-are-the-different-types-of-prosthetics/\n\n## Benefits of 3D Printing for Prosthetics\n\n3D printing technology has revolutionized the field of prosthetics by offering significant benefits such as customization, cost-effectiveness, and rapid production.
