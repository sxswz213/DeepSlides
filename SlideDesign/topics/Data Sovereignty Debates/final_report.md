Data sovereignty refers to the legal authority a nation has over data generated or processed within its borders. This means that data stored in a specific country is subject to that country's laws, which can vary significantly. For example, the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) imposes strict rules on data handling, affecting organizations worldwide, even if the data is stored outside the EU [1][3].\n\nData residency focuses on the physical location of data storage, which is essential for compliance with local laws. If a company stores data in Canada, it must adhere to Canadian data protection laws [2][4]. Data localization requires that certain types of data remain within a specific jurisdiction, complicating international data flows and increasing operational costs for businesses [1][2][3]. As countries assert control over their data, organizations must navigate these complexities to ensure compliance and protect sensitive information effectively [5].\n\n\n\n![A globe illustrating the concept of data sovereignty, with various locations marked by shield icons.](https://assets-global.website-files.com/5ff66329429d880392f6cba2/66293aabe52b998c6c680973_595-min.jpg)\n*A globe illustrating the concept of data sovereignty, with various locations marked by shield icons.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Data Sovereignty, Data Residency, and Data Localization: An Introduction.
