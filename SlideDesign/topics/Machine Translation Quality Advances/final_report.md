SMT, which emerged in the 1990s, relies on statistical models to analyze bilingual text pairs, identifying patterns for translation. While it marked an improvement over earlier rule-based systems, SMT often struggles with context and fluency, leading to awkward translations [1][5].\n\nIn contrast, NMT, which gained prominence around 2016, utilizes deep learning techniques to understand the context of entire sentences rather than translating word by word. This results in more natural and fluent translations, as NMT systems learn from vast datasets and adapt to various language pairs [2][3]. However, NMT requires substantial computational resources and may falter with nuanced or domain-specific content [4]. \n\nLooking ahead, advancements in AI-driven translation tools are expected to enhance linguistic accuracy further, integrating real-time terminology learning and adaptive neural networks [1][3].
