The three primary methods are absorption, adsorption, and membrane separation. \n\nAbsorption involves the use of solvents, typically amines, to dissolve CO₂ from gas streams. This method is widely used in industrial applications due to its effectiveness in capturing large volumes of CO₂. Adsorption, on the other hand, utilizes solid materials that attract and hold CO₂ molecules on their surface. Recent advancements in materials, such as ionic liquids and nanofluids, have enhanced the efficiency of this method, making it a promising alternative to traditional solvents.\n\nMembrane separation is an emerging technology that employs selective barriers to separate CO₂ from other gases. This method is gaining traction due to its lower energy requirements and potential for integration into existing systems. Research indicates that membrane technologies can significantly improve the efficiency of CO₂ capture processes, particularly in post-combustion scenarios.\n\nThese technologies collectively play a crucial role in reducing emissions from various sources, including power plants and industrial facilities.\n\n\n\n![Diagram illustrating a gas separation process using advanced membrane technology for CO₂ removal.](https://www.andritz.com/resource/blob/492378/c5733128e69cff234c39265cc80e09c6/aag-stackmembrane-3840px-web-data.jpg)\n*Diagram illustrating a gas separation process using advanced membrane technology for CO₂ removal.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Advanced CO2 Capture Technologies Absorption, Adsorption, and ...: https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/978-3-030-18858-0.pdf  \n[2] Absorption, Adsorption, and Membrane Separation Methods: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/345435747_Advanced_CO2_Capture_Technologies_Absorption_Adsorption_and_Membrane_Separation_Methods  \n[3] Advancements in CO2 capture by absorption and adsorption: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2212982024000623  \n[4] Membranes for CO2 capture and separation: Progress in research ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1383586623029301  \n[5] Membrane-Based Technologies for Post-Combustion CO2 Capture ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10744594/\n\n## CO₂ Conversion Processes\n\nThe conversion of captured CO₂ into useful products is a critical component of carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) technologies.
