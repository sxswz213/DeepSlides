These models are constructed using gene-protein-reaction (GPR) associations derived from genomic annotations and experimental data, allowing for the simulation of metabolic fluxes and predictions of cellular behavior under various conditions. The first GEM was developed for *Haemophilus influenzae* in 1999, and since then, the field has expanded significantly, with models now available for thousands of organisms across bacteria, archaea, and eukarya.\n\nThe construction of GEMs typically involves several key steps: annotating genes with metabolic functions, retrieving relevant biochemical data, and applying optimization techniques such as flux balance analysis (FBA) to predict metabolic outcomes. These models serve multiple purposes, including strain development for bio-based chemical production, drug target identification in pathogens, and understanding complex metabolic interactions in microbial communities. As the techniques for genome sequencing and data integration improve, GEMs are becoming increasingly valuable tools in systems biology and metabolic engineering, enabling researchers to explore metabolic pathways and their implications for health and disease.\n\n\n\n![Flowchart illustrating the process of constructing genome-scale metabolic models.](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/337410964/figure/fig1/AS:941351118057474@1601447004878/Flowchart-of-the-process-of-construction-of-genome-scale-metabolic-model-and-multi-tissue.png)\n*Flowchart illustrating the process of constructing genome-scale metabolic models.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Construction of Multiscale Genome-Scale Metabolic Models: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9139095/  \n[2] Review Emerging methods for genome-scale metabolic modeling of ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1043276024000626  \n[3] Current status and applications of genome-scale metabolic models: https://genomebiology.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13059-019-1730-3  \n[4] Fast automated reconstruction of genome-scale metabolic models ...: https://academic.oup.com/nar/article/46/15/7542/5042022  \n[5] Genome-Scale Metabolic Modeling Enables In-Depth ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8778254/\n\n## Applications of GEMs in Metabolic Engineering\n\nGenome-scale metabolic models (GEMs) are pivotal in metabolic engineering, enabling the design and optimization of microbial strains for various applications. One significant issue is the reliance on incomplete genome data, which can lead to inaccurate metabolic models. This challenge is compounded by cryptic metabolic capacities that are not fully understood, making it difficult to predict organism behavior accurately [1]. Additionally, the integration of kinetic data into GEMs remains a hurdle, as competing objectives can result in unrealistic predictions during flux balance analysis [4].\n\nFuture directions in GEM research should focus on enhancing model accuracy through improved data integration and validation techniques. The development of serum-free media and optimization of growth conditions for various strains are critical for advancing GEM applications in industrial settings [1][3]. Furthermore, leveraging computational models to simulate complex interactions within microbial communities can provide insights into strain compatibility and therapeutic efficacy [5]. \n\nBy addressing these challenges and exploring innovative methodologies, GEMs can significantly contribute to the development of tailored biotherapeutics and sustainable industrial processes.\n\n\n\n![Workflow for genome-scale metabolic model reconstruction of E. coli, showcasing challenges such as incomplete genome data and cryptic metabolic capacity.](https://www.frontiersin.org/files/Articles/1143770/fmicb-14-1143770-HTML/image_m/fmicb-14-1143770-g001.jpg)\n*Workflow for genome-scale metabolic model reconstruction of E. coli, showcasing challenges such as incomplete genome data and cryptic metabolic capacity.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Genome-scale metabolic models in cultivated meat: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0958166925000576  \n[2] Current State, Challenges, and Opportunities in Genome-Scale ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11278519/  \n[3] Genome-scale metabolic network models for industrial ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0734975024000132  \n[4] Enhancing genome-scale metabolic models with kinetic data ...: https://academic.oup.com/bioinformaticsadvances/advance-article/doi/10.1093/bioadv/vbaf166/8198051?searchresult=1  \n[5] Genome-scale metabolic model-guided systematic framework for ...: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41540-025-00555-5\n\n# Genome-Scale Metabolic Engineering\n\nGenome-scale metabolic engineering (GEM) is a transformative approach that leverages comprehensive metabolic models to optimize biological systems for various applications.
