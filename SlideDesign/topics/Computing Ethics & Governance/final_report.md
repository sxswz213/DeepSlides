The field emerged in the 1940s, primarily through the work of Norbert Wiener, who introduced the concept of cybernetics. His seminal works, including *Cybernetics* (1948) and *The Human Use of Human Beings* (1950), laid the groundwork for understanding the ethical implications of information technology and its impact on society [1][5].\n\nIn the 1970s, Walter Maner coined the term \"computer ethics\" to describe ethical issues exacerbated or created by computing technology. He emphasized the need for a distinct ethical framework to address these challenges [2][3]. The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) adopted its first code of ethics in 1973, marking a significant step in formalizing ethical standards for computing professionals [2][4].\n\nKey principles of computing ethics include privacy, intellectual property, and the responsible use of technology. As technology continues to evolve, so too do the ethical considerations surrounding it, making this field increasingly relevant in today's digital age [3][4].\n\n\n\n![A summary of the history of computing ethics, emphasizing Norbert Wiener’s pioneering work.](https://image.slideserve.com/1468630/the-brief-history-of-computer-ethics-l.jpg)\n*A summary of the history of computing ethics, emphasizing Norbert Wiener’s pioneering work.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] The history of computer ethics and its future challenges: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/285512569_The_history_of_computer_ethics_and_its_future_challenges  \n[2] Computer ethics: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_ethics  \n[3] A Very Short History of Computer Ethics: https://www.cs.utexas.edu/~ear/cs349/Bynum_Short_History.html  \n[4] Computer Ethics: Basic Concepts and Historical Overview: https://www.cs.ucdavis.edu/~rogaway/classes/188/spring06/papers/standfordencyclopedia-computerethics.pdf  \n[5] Cybernetics and the Birth of Computer Ethics | Insights: https://www.thinkbrg.com/insights/publications/cybernetics-and-the-birth-of-computer-ethics/\n\n## Key Ethical Issues in Computing\n\nThe rapid advancement of computing technologies has brought several ethical issues to the forefront, particularly concerning privacy, data security, algorithmic bias, and intellectual property rights. Effective governance frameworks provide structured approaches to manage ethical risks, compliance, and accountability in technology use.\n\nOne prominent model is the Ethical Governance Framework (EGF), which integrates ethical principles into organizational governance practices.
