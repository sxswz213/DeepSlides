Future research and development should focus on overcoming these challenges to fully realize the potential of smart material actuators in innovative applications.\n\n## Types of Smart Materials Used in Actuators\n\nSmart materials play a crucial role in the development of actuators, with dielectric elastomers, shape memory alloys, and electrostrictive materials being the most prominent types. \n\nDielectric elastomers (DEAs) are a class of electroactive polymers that exhibit significant deformation when subjected to an electric field. They are known for their lightweight, high efficiency, and rapid response times, making them suitable for applications in soft robotics and medical devices [1][2]. DEAs can achieve large strains, which allows them to function effectively as artificial muscles [3].\n\nShape memory alloys (SMAs) are metallic materials that can return to a predetermined shape when heated above a specific temperature. This property is utilized in various applications, including aerospace and automotive systems, where precise movement is essential [4]. \n\nElectrostrictive materials, on the other hand, change shape in response to an electric field, similar to dielectric elastomers, but typically exhibit different mechanical properties and energy efficiencies. These materials are often used in precision actuators and sensors [5].\n\nThe diverse properties of these smart materials enable a wide range of applications, enhancing the functionality and efficiency of actuators across various industries.\n\n\n\n![Illustration of a dielectric elastomer actuator demonstrating its deformation in response to electrical stimulation.](https://www.epfl.ch/labs/lai/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/DEA-Principle-e1570106662884.png)\n*Illustration of a dielectric elastomer actuator demonstrating its deformation in response to electrical stimulation.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Dielectric Elastomer Actuator - an overview | ScienceDirect Topics: https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/engineering/dielectric-elastomer-actuator  \n[2] Review of Dielectric Elastomer Actuators and Their Applications in ...: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/aisy.202000282  \n[3] Dielectric elastomer actuators: medical applications review: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43939-025-00225-7  \n[4] A Review of the Applications and Challenges of Dielectric Elastomer ...: https://www.mdpi.com/2075-1702/13/2/101  \n[5] Properties and Applications of Dielectric Elastomer Actuator: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/279298816_Properties_and_Applications_of_Dielectric_Elastomer_Actuator\n\n## Actuation Mechanisms\n\nSmart materials utilize various actuation mechanisms to respond to external stimuli, enabling innovative applications across multiple fields. The primary types of actuation mechanisms include electrically responsive, thermally responsive, magnetically responsive, and photoresponsive actuation.\n\nElectrically responsive materials, such as electroactive polymers, change shape or size when an electric field is applied. This mechanism is widely used in soft robotics and artificial muscles. Thermally responsive materials, like shape-memory alloys and polymers, exhibit significant dimensional changes in response to temperature variations, making them suitable for applications in actuators and sensors [2][3].\n\nMagnetically responsive materials can alter their shape or position when exposed to a magnetic field. This property is particularly useful in applications requiring precise control, such as in medical devices. Photoresponsive materials, on the other hand, respond to light stimuli, allowing for dynamic control in applications like smart windows and light-driven actuators [1][4].\n\nThese mechanisms highlight the versatility of smart materials in creating responsive systems that can adapt to their environment.\n\n\n\n![Illustration of different actuation mechanisms in smart materials, including photo-chemical, photo-electrical, and thermal responses.](https://pub.mdpi-res.com/actuators/actuators-10-00298/article_deploy/html/images/actuators-10-00298-g001.png?1636553889)\n*Illustration of different actuation mechanisms in smart materials, including photo-chemical, photo-electrical, and thermal responses.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] Advances in smart materials soft actuators on mechanisms ...: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/08927057241248028?int.sj-full-text.similar-articles.1  \n[2] Thermal-responsive smart materials for enhanced thermoelectric ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2949821X25000249  \n[3] Stimuli-responsive shape-morphing soft actuators: metrics, materials ...: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0079642525001094  \n[4] Stimuli-responsive smart materials enabled high-performance ...: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12211237/\n\n## Applications of Smart Material Actuators\n\nSmart material actuators are transforming various fields, including robotics, biomedical devices, and aerospace. In robotics, these actuators enable precise movements and adaptability, enhancing the functionality of robotic systems. For instance, soft robotic actuators made from smart materials can mimic biological movements, allowing for more natural interactions in applications such as prosthetics and rehabilitation devices [4].\n\nIn the biomedical sector, smart materials are utilized in devices that require minimal invasiveness and high precision. Shape memory alloys (SMAs) are particularly notable for their ability to change shape in response to temperature, making them ideal for stents and other implantable devices [3]. These materials can expand or contract, providing dynamic responses to physiological changes.\n\nAerospace applications also benefit significantly from smart materials. They are used in morphing wings and vibration control systems, which enhance aircraft performance and fuel efficiency [1].
