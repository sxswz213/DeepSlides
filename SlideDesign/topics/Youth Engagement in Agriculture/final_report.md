Young people are often early adopters of technology, which can lead to the integration of modern farming techniques and improved productivity. \n\nMoreover, youth engagement fosters entrepreneurship, creating job opportunities and stimulating economic growth within rural communities. One of the primary challenges is limited access to resources, including land, financial support, and technology. Many young individuals perceive agriculture as unattractive due to these constraints, often viewing it as a low-return career option compared to other employment opportunities. This perception is compounded by societal norms that regard farming as a \"poor man's\" occupation, leading to a lack of interest among youth in pursuing agricultural careers [1][2][4].\n\nAdditionally, inadequate training and education further exacerbate the issue. Many young people lack the necessary skills and knowledge to engage effectively in agricultural practices, which diminishes their confidence and willingness to participate [3][5]. The negative societal perceptions surrounding agriculture, combined with the challenges of accessing essential resources and training, create a significant barrier to youth involvement in this vital sector.\n\nTo enhance youth engagement, it is crucial to address these barriers through targeted policies and programs that promote access to resources, education, and positive perceptions of agriculture as a viable career path.\n\n\n\n![Young people engaging in agricultural training, emphasizing the need for resources and education to overcome barriers to youth participation.](https://www.accessagriculture.org/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Videos+break+barriers.png?itok=9j2VXLW6)\n*Young people engaging in agricultural training, emphasizing the need for resources and education to overcome barriers to youth participation.*\n\n\n### Sources\n[1] A Scoping Review on Youth Participation in Agriculture: Sustainable Development, Food Security, and Economic Growth. Digital tools such as mobile applications, drones, and precision farming technologies provide young farmers with access to vital information, including market prices, weather forecasts, and best practices. This access empowers them to make informed decisions, manage risks, and enhance productivity in their agricultural endeavors [1][2].\n\nMoreover, digital platforms facilitate direct connections between young farmers and consumers, eliminating intermediaries and allowing for better pricing and market access. Initiatives like the DigiFarm app in Kenya exemplify how technology can streamline agricultural processes, offering services such as input loans and farming advice [5]. Additionally, the rise of agritech startups is creating new job opportunities and fostering entrepreneurship among youth, encouraging them to innovate within the agricultural sector [3][4].\n\nDespite these advancements, challenges such as limited access to technology and digital literacy remain significant barriers.
