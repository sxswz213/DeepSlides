Each transaction is grouped into blocks, which are linked together in a chronological chain. This structure ensures that once a block is added, it cannot be altered, providing a tamper-proof record of all transactions. The decentralized nature of blockchain enhances security and transparency, as all participants in the network have access to the same information, reducing the risk of fraud and errors.\n\nThere are several types of blockchains relevant to supply chain management: public, private, consortium, and hybrid. Public blockchains are open to anyone, while private blockchains restrict access to authorized participants. Consortium blockchains are managed by a group of organizations, allowing for shared control. Hybrid blockchains combine elements of both public and private systems, offering flexibility in governance and transparency.\n\nThe key features of blockchain include immutability, transparency, and enhanced traceability, making it an ideal solution for improving supply chain efficiency and accountability.
