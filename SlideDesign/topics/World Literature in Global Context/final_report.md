The Renaissance ignited a revival of humanism, producing iconic authors such as Dante and Shakespeare.\n\nThe 20th century heralded modernist writers like James Joyce and Virginia Woolf, who challenged traditional narrative forms. Language barriers can hinder comprehension, as students may struggle with texts in unfamiliar languages, leading to misinterpretations and a reduced appreciation for cultural nuances [1]. Additionally, biases in literary selection often prioritize Western narratives, marginalizing voices from non-Western cultures and perpetuating stereotypes [4]. This bias limits students' exposure to diverse perspectives, which is essential for a comprehensive understanding of global literature.\n\nAccessibility issues also play a significant role, as not all students have equal access to resources like translated texts or academic support services [5]. To promote inclusivity, educators can adopt strategies such as providing bilingual resources, utilizing technology for translation, and fostering discussions that encourage diverse viewpoints. Engaging students in creating their narratives and exploring literature from their cultural backgrounds can enhance understanding and appreciation [2].
