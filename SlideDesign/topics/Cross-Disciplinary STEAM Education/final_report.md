This approach encourages students to ask questions like, \"How can I customize a solution to fit this problem?\" which nurtures their ability to think flexibly and creatively.\n\nAdditionally, cross-disciplinary learning promotes collaboration among students. One significant obstacle is the lack of resources and funding. Many schools struggle to acquire necessary materials such as robotics kits and 3D printers, which are essential for effective STEAM learning. Limited budgets often lead to inadequate training for teachers, who may feel unprepared to integrate new technologies into their classrooms. This gap in training can hinder the successful implementation of STEAM initiatives, as teachers need ongoing support and professional development to build their confidence and skills.\n\nCurriculum alignment is another challenge. Educators often find it difficult to incorporate STEAM activities into an already packed curriculum, leading to a perception that STEAM is an add-on rather than an integral part of education. To address these issues, schools can adopt flexible, project-based learning units that allow for real-world problem-solving and collaboration across disciplines.
