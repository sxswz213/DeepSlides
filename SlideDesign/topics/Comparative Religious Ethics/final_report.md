Christianity emphasizes love and compassion, encapsulated in the teachings of Jesus Christ, who advocated for loving one's neighbor and even one's enemies. The core ethical principles are derived from the Bible, particularly the Ten Commandments and the Sermon on the Mount, which stress the importance of forgiveness and charity [1][3].\n\nIn Islam, ethical conduct is guided by the Quran and the Hadith, which outline the Five Pillars of Islam. These pillars promote justice, compassion, and community welfare, emphasizing the importance of charity (Zakat) and prayer (Salat) as means to cultivate moral character [3][4]. \n\nBuddhism, on the other hand, focuses on the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path, which guide adherents toward ethical living and the alleviation of suffering. For instance, the Christian tradition has developed the Just War Theory, emphasizing that war should only be waged as a last resort and must be conducted with proportionality and discrimination to minimize harm to civilians [1][3].
