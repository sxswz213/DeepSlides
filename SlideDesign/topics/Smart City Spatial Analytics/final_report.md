In transportation, cities utilize geospatial data to optimize traffic flow and reduce congestion. For instance, real-time traffic monitoring systems analyze data from sensors and cameras to adjust traffic signals dynamically, improving commute times and reducing emissions [1].\n\nIn energy management, spatial analytics helps in monitoring energy consumption patterns and identifying areas for efficiency improvements. Smart grids leverage geospatial data to manage energy distribution effectively, ensuring that supply meets demand while minimizing waste [2]. \n\nPublic health is another sector benefiting from spatial analytics. By mapping disease outbreaks and health service accessibility, cities can allocate resources more effectively and respond to public health emergencies swiftly [3]. \n\nEnvironmental monitoring also relies on spatial analytics to track pollution levels and manage natural resources. For example, while platforms like Tableau and Power BI excel in data visualization, they may not integrate spatial data as effectively as specialized GIS platforms.\n\nEmerging methodologies, such as geospatial big data analytics, enhance predictive capabilities, allowing cities to proactively tackle urban challenges like traffic congestion and air quality.
